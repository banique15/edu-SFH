## **Objectives**

**In this curriculum, students will:**  
*Knowledge*

* Develop a comprehensive understanding of Global Goal 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure) and its critical importance for achieving sustainable development and improving human wellbeing globally. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)   
* Gain awareness of the fundamental interconnectedness between robust and equitable infrastructure, inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and the fostering of innovation as drivers of progress. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Explore the specific targets within Global Goal 9, including the development of quality,  resilient infrastructure, promoting sustainable and inclusive industrial practices, enhancing scientific research, and increasing access to technology and the internet. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la)   
* Learn about the diverse challenges and opportunities associated with achieving Global Goal 9 in different communities and countries, recognizing varying levels of development and capacity. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop foundational knowledge of various creative disciplines, including drama, visual art, creative writing, dance, and music, and their potential as powerful tools for exploring and communicating complex themes related to development and progress. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la) 

*Discourse*

* Analyze and interpret diverse forms of creative expression (drama, visual art, poetry, music, dance.) to identify and critically examine underlying themes, powerful messages, and varied perspectives related to infrastructure, industrialization, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)   
* Engage thoughtfully in nuanced discussions about complex issues related to infrastructure development, sustainable industrialization, technological innovation, and their societal and environmental impacts, demonstrating respect for diverse viewpoints.(NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Articulate their own devleoping ideas, insightful perspectives, and critical reflections on the challenges and opportunities within Global Goal 9 through both clear verbal and impactful nonverbal communication. (NCCAS: Pr5.1.la)   
* Collaborate effectively and creatively with peers to generate, develop, and share meaningful artistic projects that explore and respond to the multifaceted themes of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la) 

*Attitudes*

* Cultivate a strong sense of responsibility for promoting sustainable development and contributing to a more equitable, resilient, and prosperous future where infrastructure, industry, and innovation benefit all. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop a deepened appreciation for the transformative role of resilient infrastructure, sustainable industry, and continuous innovation in improving people's lives, fostering economic progress, and protecting the planet. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la)   
* Foster a robust sense of global citizenship and a personal commitment to taking informed and creative action to achieve the Global Goals, recognizing the interconnectedness of global challenges and solutions. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 

*Capacity*

* Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, problem-solving, and articulate communication skills through active and engaged participation in a variety of creative activities and collaborative projects focused on Global Goal 9 themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)    
* Develop their analytical abilities to interpret information and diverse perspectives from various sources, including dramatic scenes, visual artwork, movement sequences, musical compositions, creative writing, and real-world examples related to infrastructure, industry, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la)    
* Strengthen their capacity for meaningful self-expression and impactful creative communication through the thoughtful and purposeful use of various artistic mediums to explore and respond to the complexities of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la)    
* Gain increased confidence in their ability to contribute to positive change and advocate for sustainable development and innovation within their communities and the wider world using artistic means. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la) 

*Action*

* Identify age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote the principles and targets of Global Goal 9 within their schools, communities, and beyond, considering both individual choices and collective advocacy. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creative skills and artistic voices to raise awareness, advocate for sustainable development, and inspire innovation that benefits all. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)    
* Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a more sustainable and innovative future for all, fostering a sense of sustained engagement and responsible global citizenship. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 

## **Echoes of Innovation: Exploring Sustainable Futures through Ancient Greek Theater**  
**(30-60 MINUTES)**  
**Materials**  
Classroom or performance space with ample room for movement and staging  
Simple staging elements (e.g., chairs, tables, risers, etc.)  
Simple costumes and props to enhance the theatrical experience (optional)  
Whiteboard or large chart paper  
Markers  
Notebooks or paper  
Pens/Pencils

	Guiding Questions

* What do you notice about these images/videos?  
* What do you already know about Ancient Greek theater?  
* What do you think it would have been like to attend a play in ancient Greece?

Facilitate a discussion about the key elements and characteristics of Ancient Greek theater.  
	Key Points

* Origins in religious rituals and festivals.  
* Performed in open-air amphitheaters.  
* Use of masks to represent characters and emotions.  
* Role of the chorus in narrating, commenting, and interacting with the actors.  
* Focus on tragedy (serious plays exploring human suffering and fate) and comedy (humorous plays often satirizing social and political issues).

	Guiding Questions

* Why do you think masks were used in Ancient Greek theater?  
* What do you think the role of the chorus was?  
* What are some of the themes or topics that were explored in Ancient Greek plays?

Introduce/re-introduce Gloibal Goal 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure. Explain the core idea of building resilient infrastructure, promoting inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and fostering innovation.  
Briefly introduce the different targets within Global Goal 9, highlighting their interconnectedness and importance.	  
Guiding Questions

* What are some of the challenges in achieving this goal/these targets?  
* How might these targets affect different people and communities around the world?

**Exploring Ancient Greek Theater Techniques**  
Discuss the significance of masks in Ancient Greek theater.  
*Symbolic Meaning:* Explore how masks were used to represent different characters, emotions, and social roles.  
*Voice and Movement:* Discuss how actors used their voices and bodies to convey emotions and character traits while wearing masks.  
*Mask-Making Activity:* **If desired and time allows**, have students create their own masks using simple materials, encouragin them to think about the characters and emotions they want to portray. You can provide masks or continue the lesson without masks if there is not time.  
	Guiding Questions

* How do masks affect the way we perceive characters and their emotions?  
* How can you use your voice and body to express yourself while wearing a mask?

Discuss the role of the chorus in Ancient Greek theater.  
*Narration and Commentary:* Explain how the chorus provided background information, commentary on the action, and insights into the characters’ thoughts and feelings.  
*Interaction with Actors:* Discuss how the chorus interacted with the actors, sometimes offering advice, expressing opinions, or participating in the action.  
*Choral Speaking Activity:* **If desired and time allows**, have students practice choral speaking, reciting lines from an Ancient Greek play or creating their own choral verses related to Global Goal 9\.  
	Guiding Questions

* How does the chorus contribute to the storytelling in Ancient Greek theater?  
* How can choral speaking be used to create a sense of unity, rhythm, or emotional impact?

Discuss the two main genres of Ancient Greek theater: tragedy and comedy.  
*Tragedy:* Explore the characteristics of tragedy, including serious themes, flawed heroes, and often tragic endings. Discuss how tragedies explored human suffering, fate, and moral dilemmas.  
*Comedy:* Explore the characteristics of comedy, including humor, satire, and often happy endings. Discuss how comedies often poked fun at social and political issues, providing a form of social commentary.  
	Gudiing Questions

* What are some of the differences between tragedy and comedy?  
* How can both tragedy and comedy be used to address important social issues?

**Ancient Greek Theater and Global Goal 9**  
Choose one of the following short plays (or another suitable Ancient Greek theater-inspired play related to Global Goal 9\) for students to perform. The class can also split up into groups and perform multiple plays if desired.  
[The Bridge Builders (Tragedy)](https://docs.google.com/document/d/16tkwj2bysA9ijFLLEzc055hP0LJytPhPfBUYfc6ED9g/edit?usp=drive_link)  
[The Innovation Race (Comedy)](https://docs.google.com/document/d/101RirdQp81tVtT_H-Zk0ztaYxjn2VZAORZK7bjbvs78/edit?usp=drive_link)  
[The Sustainable City (Tragedy/Comedy)](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1uYgr6gKHqYR53M3NZeHMkqQ2PZXkRnllHyr6X7X58Sc/edit?usp=drive_link)  
Divide students into groups and assign roles based on their interests and abilities.  
Allow time for students to rehearse, develop their characters, and plan performances.  
Encourage students to utilize simple costuming, props, and masks to enhance their presentations.  
**Performances and Discussion**  
Have students perform their plays for the class. Encourage the audience to actively listen and observe, paying attention to the messages, emotions, and theatrical techniques used in each performance.  
Facilitate a class discussion about the plays, focusing on the connection to Global Goal 9, the effectiveness of the Ancient Greek theater techniques, and the overall impact of the performances.  
	Guiding Questions

* What were some of the most powerful or memorable moments in the plays?  
* How did these plays make you think differently about Global Goal 9?  
* What were some of the common themes or messages you noticed in the plays?  
* How did the use of masks, chorus, tragedy, and comedy enhance the storytelling and impact of the performances?


  
**Reflection and Summary**  
Have students reflect on their learning experience through discussion or writing.  
Ask guiding questions like:

* How did exploring Ancient Greek theater techniques \- like the use of masks, the chorus, or the contrast between tragedy and comedy \- help you understand the themes of Global Goal 9?  
* What did you learn about the power of these ancient theatrical forms to communicate complex ideas about industry, innovation, and infrastructure, even today?  
* Did using masks, or the idea of a chorus, change how you perceived or portrayed the different voices and perspectives related to Global Goal 9?  
* How did the plays’ narrative, whether tragic or comedic, make you think differently about the challenges or opportunities in achieving resilient infrastructure or inclusive industrialization?   
* What did you discover about the connection between storytelling and human progress through this exploration of ancient theater?  
* What new insights did you gain about Global Goal 9 and the importance of sustainable development in industry, innovation, and infrastructure?  
* How did the themes and conflicts within the plays connect to specific targets or broader challenges within Global Goal 9?  
* Did seeing these issues dramatized make them feel more urgent or relatable than just reading facts and figures?  
* How does the historical context of Greek theater, which often addressed societal issues, relate to using the arts today to advocate for goals like Global Goal 9?  
* Did any particular scene, character, or theatrical element from the plays resonate with you personally regarding Global Goal 9?  
* What emotions did you experience while participating in or watching the performances, and how might those emotions motivate you to take action?  
* How has this lesson influenced your thinking about your own role in supporting innovation or advocating for sustainable infrastructure in your community?

**Assessment**

* Consistent and enthusiastic involvement in all phases of the lesson, including the introduction to Greek theater, technique explorations, play rehearsals, and class discussions.  
* Comprehension of Global Goal 9 and the connection to industry, innovation, and infrastructure, as demonstrated through the interpretation of characters/chorus and their explanations during discussion.  
* Originality and effectiveness in applying chosen Greek theater elements (e.g., use of masks, choral speaking, tragic/comic tone) to convey messages related to Global Goal 9 in their scenes.  
* How clearly and powerfully the scene communicated its intended message about Global Goal 9\. Look for the ability of the performance to evoke thought or emotion in the audience.  
* Ability to work effectively within groups, contributing ideas, supporting scene partners, and working as a cohesive ensemble (especially in choral elements).  
* Nuance and insight demonstrated in the reflections, particularly concerning the role of ancient theater in addressing modern global challenges and the complexities of achieving sustainable development.
