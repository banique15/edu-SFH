**Exploring Satirical Techniques**  
*Irony*  
Definition: Where the intended meaning is the opposite of what is explicitly stated.  
	Examples

* A character praising a corrupt politician while subtly highlighting their flaws.  
* A scene depicting a “fair” competition where the outcome is clearly rigged in favor of the privileged.

	Guiding Questions

* How does irony create humor and social commentary in satire?  
* What are some different types of irony? (e.g., verbal irony, situational irony, dramatic irony)

*Exaggeration*   
Definition: The use of exaggeration or hyperbole to emphasize and ridicule social absurdities.  
	Examples

* A character obsessed with wealth and status, to the point of absurdity.  
* A scene depicting a society where the rich live in extravagant luxury while the poor struggle to survive.

	Guiding Questions

* How does exaggeration help to expose and critique social problems?  
* How can exaggeration be used to create humor and engage the audience?

*Humor*  
The role of humor in satire, emphasizing that it can be used to make a serious point and encourage critical thinking.  
	Examples

* Using witty dialogue or slapstick comedy to highlight social inequalities.  
* Creating absurd or paradoxical situations to expose the flaws in social systems.

	Guiding Questions

* How does humor make satire more engaging and accessible to audiences?  
* How can humor be used to challenge power structures and promote social change?

*Social Commentary*  
Definition: How satire uses humor and exaggeration to provide social commentary and critique social norms, values, and institutions.  
	Examples

* A skit satirizing the unequal distribution of wealth and resources.  
* A scene critiquing discriminatory practices or policies.

	Guiding Questions

* How does satire encourage us to question the status quo and consider alternative perspectives?  
* How can satire be used to inspire action and promote social change?

**Satire and Global Goal 10**  
Facilitate a brainstorming session where students generate ideas for satirical skits that connect to the different targets within Global Goal 10\.  
Feel free to give them ideas as a jumping off point.  
Examples (Can give to students as a jumping off point)

* **Target 10.1** *(Income Growth):* A skit satirizing the extreme wealth gap, where the rich live in opulent excess while the poor struggle to afford basic necessities.  
* **Target 10.2** *(Social Inclusion):* A skit portraying a society where certain groups are excluded from opportunities and resources based on their race, gender, or other characteristics.  
* **Target 10.3** *(Equal Opportunity):* A skin satirizing a workplace where promotions and opportunities are based on favoritism and connections rather than merit.  
* **Target 10.4** *(Social Protection):* A skit highlighting the inadequacy of social safety nets and the challenges faced by vulnerable populations.  
* **Target 10.5** *(Financial Markets):* A skit satirizing the greed and corruption in the financial industry, where profits are prioritized over ethical considerations.  
* **Target 10.6** *(Global Institutions):* A skit portraying the unequal power dynamics in global decision-making, where the voices of developing countries are often marginalized.  
* **Target 10.7** *(Migration):* A skit satirizing the challenges faced by migrants and refugees, highlighting the need for more humane and inclusive migration policies.  
* **Target 10.a** *(Special and Differential Treatment):* A skit critiquing unfair trade practices that disadvantage developing countries.  
* **Target 10.b** *(Development Assistance):* A skit satirizing the disparity in development aid distribution, where some countries receive disproportionate amounts of assistance while others are neglected.  
* **Target 10.c** *(Remittances):* A skit highlighting the high transaction costs associated with remittances and the impact on migrant workers and their families.

	Guiding Questions

* What are some social or economic inequalities that could be satirized in a skit?  
* How can we use satirical techniques (irony, exaggeration, humor) to expose and critique these inequalities?  
* How can we use satire to promote understanding and empathy for marginalized groups?

Divide students into smaller groups. Provide each group with a pre-written satirical script that connects to Global Goal 10\. Guide students in rehearsing their short skits, incorporating the techniques discussed earlier (irony, exaggeration, humor). Encourage students to use simple costumes, props, and visual aids to enhance their perofrmances (if available).

**Performance and Discussion**  
Have each group perform their skit for the class.  
Encourage the audience to actively listen and observe, paying attention to the satirical techniques used and the messages being conveyed.  
Facilitate a class discussion about the skits, focusing on the connection to Global Goal 10, the effectiveness of the satire, and the overall impact of the performances.  
	Guiding Questions

* What were some of the most effective or memorable satirical techniques used in the skits?  
* How did these skits make you think differently about inequality and social justice?  
* What were some of the common themes or challenges that emerged in the skits?  
* How did the use of humor and exaggeration help to convey serious messages about inequality?


  
**Reflection and Summary**  
Have students reflect on their learning experience.  
Guiding Questions

* What new insights did you gain about Global Goal 10 and the importance of reducing inequality?  
* How did exploring satire deepen your understanding of social justice and the power of humor to address serious issues?  
* How can satire be used to raise awareness and inspire action on global issues?  
* What are some specific actions you can take to contribute to achieving Global Goal 10?

**Assessment**

* Quality of student skits and performances, considering creativity, originality, effective use of satirical techniques, and connection to Global Goal 10\.  
* Active participation in discussions, group activities, and the sharing of skits.  
* Understanding of Global Goal 10 targets and their ability to connect them to their skits and discussions.
