## MUSICAL EXPLORATION
**(10-30 MINUTES) (GRADES 5-12)**

**Materials**
**
	Speaker or computer for audio playback

Lyrics Sheets (optional and dependent on the song chosen) OR projected lyrics

Images or short video clips depicting the impacts of climate change (e.g., melting glaciers, extreme weather events, deforestation) and climate action (e.g., renewable energy, community gardens, sustainable transportation) [optional, for visual context]

**Song Examples**

Give Peace a Chance, John Lennon

A Change Is Gonna Come, Sam Cooke

Get Up, Stand Up, Bob Marley & The Wailers

Where Is the Love?, The Black Eyed Peas

The Times They Are A-Changin’, Bob Dylan

Imagine, John Lennon

War, Edwin Starr

Zombie, The Cranberries

Sunday Bloody Sunday, U2

Luka, Suzanne Vega

Dirty Deeds Done Dirty Cheap, ACDC

What’s Going On, Marvin Gaye

Land of Confusion, Genesis

Blowin’ in the Wind, Bob Dylan

**Discussion**

- Ask students to reflect on the lyrics, melody, and overall mood of the song(s)
- What emotions do you feel when you hear this song?
- What are the main messages or themes that these songs convey about peace, justice and society?
- How do the musical elements (melody, rhythm, harmony, dynamics) contribute to the overall mood and message of the song? For example, does the music sound hopeful, angry, determined, or reflective?
- How might the artist’s background or the time period in which the song was written influence its message about peace and justice?
- How does this song(s) relate to the idea of promoting peaceful and inclusive societies and providing access to justice (Global Goal 16)?
- Can you connect specific lyrics or musical elements to any of the targets within Global Goal 16? For example, does a song address violence (Target 16.1), the need for justice (Target 16.3), or inclusivity (Target 16.7)?
- What are some of the challenges and opportunities associated with achieving peace and justice in the world that these songs might highlight?
- How do these songs make you think differently about your role in creating a more peaceful and just society?

Emphasize how music can be a powerful tool for expressing emotions, raising awareness, and inspiring action around issues of peace, justice, and human rights.

**Personal Connection**

Guiding Questions

- What does peace mean to you personally? What does justice mean?
- Have you ever witnessed or experienced situations where peace or justice were absent? How did that make you feel?
- What are some expamples of people or groups working for peace and justice in your community or the world?
- What are some ways that young people like you can contribute to creating a more peaceful and inclusive society?
- How can understanding the importance of justice impact how we treat others and participate in our communities?

Encourage students to share their personal experiences and connect them to the broader themes explored in the song(s).

**Closing Activity Options**

	**One Word Summary:** Ask each student to think of one word encapsulating their biggest takeaway or feeling about peace and justice after this musical exploration. Have them share their word with the class, briefly explaining their choice. **Bonus:** Collect all the words to make a word cloud.

	**Quote Connection:** Share an inspiring quote about peace, justice, or human rights. Facilitate an open discussion about how this quote connects to the music and activity they experienced in class. **Example:** “Darkness cannot drive out darkness: only light can do that. Hate cannot drive out hate: only love can do that.” - Martin Luther King Jr. 

	**Songwriting Challenge:** Divide students into small groups and challenge them to write a short song or rap verse about peace, justice, or inclusivity. Encourage them to consider the challenges, solutions, and the importance of achieving Global Goal 16.

	**Global Goal Playlist:** Have students work individually or in groups to brainstorm other songs that they think relate to different aspects of Global Goal 16. They can briefly explain their choices.

	**Creative Writing Connection:** Have students write a short story, poem, or song lyrics inspired by the music and discussions, focusing on thei feelings or ideas about peace, justice, and strong institutions.

**Visual Art Connection:** Have students create a quick sketch or image that comes to mind when they listen to the song(s) in the context of Global Goal 16. They can share their artwork and explain the connection.

**Soundscape of Justice:** Divide students into small groups and challenge them to create a short soundscape (using their voices, body percussion, or classroom objects) that represents either the challenges in achieving peace, justice, and inclusivity or actions being taken to address these challenges.
