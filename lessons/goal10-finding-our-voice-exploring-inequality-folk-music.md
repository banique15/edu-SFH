## **Finding Our Voice: Exploring Inequality Through Folk Music**  
**(30-60 MINUTES)**  
**Materials**  
Music classroom or a space with ample room for movement and sound exploration  
Access to a variety of simple instruments (percussion, harmonicas, recorders, ukeleles, hand drums, etc.) or classroom objects that can be used as instruments  
Whiteboard or projector  
Markers  
Notebooks or paper  
Pens/Pencils  
Audio  
Lyrics sheets and/or sheet music for folk songs related to Global Goal 10

**Introduction**  
Begin with a musical icebreaker to get students engaged and thinking about the power of music to tell stories and express emotions.  
	Example

* Play a game where students take turns singing or humming a familiar song, and others guess the song title.

Facilitate a discussion about the characteristics of folk music.  
	Key Points

* Traditional music often passed down orally through generations.  
* Tells stories about everyday life, social issues, and historical events.  
* Uses simple melodies and harmonies, making it accessible for diverse voices and instruments.  
* Often associated with social justice movements and protests.

	Guiding Questions

* What are some examples of folk music that you have heard?  
* Why do you think folk music has been such an important part of different cultures throughout history?  
* How can music be used to tell stories, express emotions, or bring people together?

Introduce/re-introduce Global Goal 10: Reduced Inequalities. Explain the core idea of reducing inequality within and among countries, and ensuring equal opportunities and access to resources for all.  
Briefly introduce the different targets within Global Goal 10, highlighting their interconnectedness and importance.  
	Guiding Questions

* What are some of the causes and consequences of inequality?  
* How does inequality affect different people and communities around the world?  
* What can we do to create a more just and equitable world?  
* What might a world with less inequality look like?  
* Think about your own community, where do you see examples of inequality?  
* Have you ever felt excluded or treated unfairly because of your background, identity, or circumstances?  
* What role do you think music can play in addressing social issues like inequality?  
* What are some of the systems or structures in our society that might contribute to inequality?  
* Is inequality always a bad thing? Can there be any positive aspects to inequality? (This can lead to a discussion about equity vs. equality).

**Exploring Folk Music**  
Play examples of folk music from different cultures and time periods  
	Examples

* [“This Land Is Your Land” Woody Guthrie](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XaI5IRuS2aE), American folk song about social justice and equality.  
* [“The Times They Are A-Changin’” Bob Dylan](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8b3RqnzgH1E), protest song addressing themes of war, peace, and freedom.  
* [“We Shall Overcome” Morehouse College Glee Club](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Aor6-DkzBJ0), traditional gospel song associated with the Civil Rights movement.  
* [“If I Had a Hammer” Pete Seeger](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VO39e5Uznu4), folk song about social change and activism  
* [“Gracias a la Vida” Victoria Parra](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w67-hlaUSIs), Chilean folk song celebrating life and resilience  
* [“Deportee (Plane Wreck at Los Gatos)” Woody Guthrie](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qu-duTWccyI), Tells the tragic story of a plane crash that killed migrant workers.  
* [“Strange Fruit” Billie Holiday](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-DGY9HvChXk), A haunting protest song about the lynching of Black Americans in the South, exposing the brutal reality of racial inequality and injustice.  
* [“Where Did You Sleep Last Night (In The Pines)”,](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5tfPHBRIS14) Nirvana, a haunting American folk song with mysterious origins and lyrics that vary widely from version to version (and has been performed by many artists), often featuring themes of loss, hardship, and death.  
* [“El Pueblo Unido Jamás Será Vencido (The People United Will Never Be Defeated)” Chilean folk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kTLrFjYt8tA), A powerful anthem of resistance and unity against oppression.  
* [“Bella Ciao” Italian Partisan Song](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zyh_OVeQZH0), A song of resistance and defiance associated with anti-fascist movements in Italy and beyond.

	Guiding Questions

* What are some of the common themes or messages in the songs?  
* How do the lyrics and music work together to create an emotional impact?  
* How do these songs connect to the idea of reducing inequality and promoting social justice?

Teach students a simple folk song related to Global Goal 10\.  
	Examples

* [This Land is Your Land](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1VLcEYuTpGX7Im8MWWtT1RHZAl3OndrUY/view?usp=drive_link)  
* [In the Pines](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1GoUHKuwF93FGc3YA6brPdSt7ePOQQLkr9E9S-jGvOJo/edit?usp=drive_link)  
* [If I Had A Hammer](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1FBph5D_9y8gY2thECwIaFct5X7z6yJ_p/view?usp=drive_link)

Start by teaching the melody and lyrics, then add simple rhythmic accompaniment usign clapping, snapping, or percussion instruments.  
Encourage students to sing together, creating a sense of unity and shared purpose.  
If desired and timing allows, introduce students to simple instruments that are commonly used in folk music (harmonica, hand drums, ukelele, etc.).  
Allow students to experiment with the instruments and learn basic chords or rhythms to accompany the song.  
**Singing Together**  
Have the entire class practice and perform the chosen folk song together, with optional instrumental accompaniment.  
Explore different moods and interpretations each time the class sings the song.  
*Dynamics:* Sing the song softly at first, then gradually increase the volume to create a sense of growing power and determination.  
*Tempo:* Start with a slow tempo, the gradually speed up to create a sense of urgency and action.  
*Harmony:* Divide the class into two or three groups and have them sing different harmonies to create a richer and more complex sound.  
*Adding Percussion:* Use simple percussion instruments to creat a rhythmic accompaniment that enhances the energy and message of the song.  
*Call and Response:* Have one group sing a line of phrase, and the rest of the class echo it back, creating a sense of unity and participation.  
	Guiding Questions

* How did it feel to sing this song together?  
* What emotions or ideas did the song evoke in you?  
* How does music connect to the themes of Global Goal 10 and the goal of reducing inequalities?


  
**Reflection and Summary**  
Have students reflect on their learning experience through discussion or writing.  
Ask guiding questions like:

* How did exploring the characteristics of folk music \- its simplicity, storytelling, and focus on everyday life \- help you connect with the complexities of reducing inequalities?  
* What did you learn about the power of music to amplify voices and unite people, especially in the context of fighting social injustices?  
* Did singing together as a class, or playing simple instruments, create a sense of community or shared purpose that relates to achieving Global Goal 10?  
* How did analyzing the lyrics and musical elements of protest songs deepen your understanding of the issues of inequality they addressed?  
* What did you discover about how music can transcend language barriers to convey emotions and messages about justice and equality?  
* What new insights did you gain about Global Goal 10 and the importance of reducing inequalities within and among countries?  
* How did hearing folk music from different cultures or time periods broaden your understanding of the diverse forms and impacts of inequality globally?  
* What common themes or calls to action for a more just and equitable world emerged across the different folk songs we explored?  
* How does the history of folk music as a tool for social change inform your understanding of its potential for addressing modern inequalities?  
* Did this lesson make you reflect on examples of inequality in your own community or the wider world in a new way?  
* Did any particular folk song or the act of making music resonate with your own experiences or feelings about fairness and equality?  
* What emotions did you connect with during this lesson, and how might those emotions motivate you to take action related to Global Goal 10?  
* How can you use your voice, whether through music or other forms of expression, to advocate for a more equitable world?

**Assessment**

* Consistent and enthusiastic involvement in all phases of the lesson, including the rhythm circle, song analysis, collaborative singing, and discussions.  
* Comprehension of Global Goal 10, its targets and connections to reducing inequalities, as demonstrated through analysis of folk song messages and contributions to rhythmic chants/songs.  
* Originality and effectiveness in creating rhythmic chants or songs, using body percussion or simple instruments to convey messages about inequality and social justice.  
* Ability to critically listen to and analyze folk songs, identifying how lyrics and musical elements contribute to the overall message and emotional impact related to Global Goal 10\.  
* Willingness to work effectively with peers, listen to others’ musical contributions, and collaborate constructively during group activities and whole-class singing.  
* Nuance and insight demonstrated in reflections, particularly concerning the role of music in addressing social justice issues and the complexities of achieving a more equitable world.
