## **ART EXPLORATION**

**(10-30 MINUTES) (GRADES 5-12)**  
**Materials**  
	Images or prints of artwork depicting themes of education, equality, and social justice  
	Large paper or poster board for each student/group  
	Variety of art supplies  
		Markers, paints, colored pencils, charcoal, pastels  
		Collage materials: magazines, newspapers, fabric scraps, etc.  
		Other mixed media options: yarn beads, found objects, etc.  
	Scissors and glue (for collage option)  
**Art Examples**	  
[The Problem We All Live With](https://www.thisamericanlife.org/562/the-problem-we-all-live-with-part-one), Norman Rockwell  
	Depicts a young black girl escorted to school by federal marshals during desegregation, highlighting the struggle for equal access to education.  
[School of Athens](https://www.britannica.com/topic/School-of-Athens), Raphael  
	This Renaissance masterpiece celebrates the pursuit of knowledge and philosophy, inspiring students to appreciate the value of education.  
[The Dinner Party](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=5150c1708da75793&q=the+dinner+party+judy+chicago&udm=2&fbs=AEQNm0Aa4sjWe7Rqy32pFwRj0UkWd8nbOJfsBGGB5IQQO6L3J_86uWOeqwdnV0yaSF-x2jpXXSZVlK6C0YPjHbsLu8HQlFjXJu4aVhI_llTnXJ4lFAWdNYSKl18X_OYOML0jevhpDEumYRwaaY1jEa7vKdTgiN-XUHVrwULe1SBpdZ2b2Qdf9JCr6vszwGWtXx6BBaAiRVuZU26XGXcLhLP1MT26u-HDMw&sa=X&sqi=2&ved=2ahUKEwjxueHNiviHAxUg78kDHdYEBAgQtKgLegQIEBAB&biw=1200&bih=694&dpr=2#vhid=O6h69IFiyGcJWM&vssid=mosaic), Judy Chicago  
	This feminist installation artwork celebrates the achievements of women throughout history, inspiring discussions about gender equality in education.  
[The Reading Lesson](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Jean-Baptiste_Greuze_-_Reading_the_bible.jpg), Jean-Baptiste Greuze  
	Depicts a father teaching his children to read, emphasizing the importance of family involvement in education.  
[The Library](https://americanart.si.edu/artwork/library-14376), Jacob Lawrence  
	Portrays a bustling library filled with people of all ages and backgrounds, illustrating the power of libraries as centers of learning and community.  
[Literacy](https://www.bridgemanimages.com/en-US/rivera/literacy-by-diego-rivera-1886-1957-detail-from-the-ministry-of-education-frescoes-1923-1928-mexico/nomedium/asset/1254656), Diego Rivera  
	Part of a series of murals, this work showcases the importance of education and literacy for social progress and empowerment.  
**Viewing**

* Allow students about 5 minutes to engage with the chosen piece, giving the class more or less preamble/guidance about the artwork depending on their needs.

**Discussion**

* What emotions does this artwork evoke in you?  
* What messages or stories do you think the artists are trying to convey?  
* How do these artworks relate to the concept of education and the goals of Global Goal 4?  
* Can you identify any specific part of the artwork that resonates with a particular target of Global Goal 4? How so?  
* How does the artwork illustrate the challenges or successes in achieving quality education for all?  
* What symbols or visual elements stand out to you and what might they represent in the context of education?

Emphasize how art can be a powerful tool for expressing ideas, challenging perspectives, and inspiring action around social issues like education.  
**Personal Connection**

* Ask students to reflect on their own experiences with education.  
* Have you ever faced barriers to accessing quality education?  
* Have you witnessed any inequalities or injustices in your school or community related to education?  
* What are your hopes and dreams for your own education and future learning?

Encourage students to share their personal stories and connect them to the themes explored in the artwork.  
**Closing Activity Options**  
	**One Word Summary:** Ask each student to think of one word encapsulating their biggest takeaway or feeling about edcuation after this art exploration. Have them share their word with the class, briefly explaining their choice. **Bonus:** Collect all the words to make a word cloud.  
	**Quote Connection:** Share an inspiring quote about education. Facilitate an open discussion about how this quote connects to the music and activity they experienced in class. **Example:** "Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world." \- Nelson Mandela  
	**Artwork Challenge:** Divide students into small groups and challenge them to create a "flash art" project with mixed media in 10 minutes. Encourage them to consider the challenges, solutions, and the importance of achieving quality education for all.  
	**Global Goal Gallery:** Have students work in groups to find other artwork that represents different aspects of Global Goal 3 and resonates with them.
