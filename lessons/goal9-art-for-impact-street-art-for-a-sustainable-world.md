## **Objectives**

**In this curriculum, students will:**  
*Knowledge*

* Develop a comprehensive understanding of Global Goal 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure) and its critical importance for achieving sustainable development and improving human wellbeing globally. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)   
* Gain awareness of the fundamental interconnectedness between robust and equitable infrastructure, inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and the fostering of innovation as drivers of progress. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Explore the specific targets within Global Goal 9, including the development of quality,  resilient infrastructure, promoting sustainable and inclusive industrial practices, enhancing scientific research, and increasing access to technology and the internet. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la)   
* Learn about the diverse challenges and opportunities associated with achieving Global Goal 9 in different communities and countries, recognizing varying levels of development and capacity. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop foundational knowledge of various creative disciplines, including drama, visual art, creative writing, dance, and music, and their potential as powerful tools for exploring and communicating complex themes related to development and progress. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la) 

*Discourse*

* Analyze and interpret diverse forms of creative expression (drama, visual art, poetry, music, dance.) to identify and critically examine underlying themes, powerful messages, and varied perspectives related to infrastructure, industrialization, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)   
* Engage thoughtfully in nuanced discussions about complex issues related to infrastructure development, sustainable industrialization, technological innovation, and their societal and environmental impacts, demonstrating respect for diverse viewpoints.(NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Articulate their own devleoping ideas, insightful perspectives, and critical reflections on the challenges and opportunities within Global Goal 9 through both clear verbal and impactful nonverbal communication. (NCCAS: Pr5.1.la)   
* Collaborate effectively and creatively with peers to generate, develop, and share meaningful artistic projects that explore and respond to the multifaceted themes of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la) 

*Attitudes*

* Cultivate a strong sense of responsibility for promoting sustainable development and contributing to a more equitable, resilient, and prosperous future where infrastructure, industry, and innovation benefit all. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop a deepened appreciation for the transformative role of resilient infrastructure, sustainable industry, and continuous innovation in improving people's lives, fostering economic progress, and protecting the planet. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la)   
* Foster a robust sense of global citizenship and a personal commitment to taking informed and creative action to achieve the Global Goals, recognizing the interconnectedness of global challenges and solutions. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 

*Capacity*

* Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, problem-solving, and articulate communication skills through active and engaged participation in a variety of creative activities and collaborative projects focused on Global Goal 9 themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)    
* Develop their analytical abilities to interpret information and diverse perspectives from various sources, including dramatic scenes, visual artwork, movement sequences, musical compositions, creative writing, and real-world examples related to infrastructure, industry, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la)    
* Strengthen their capacity for meaningful self-expression and impactful creative communication through the thoughtful and purposeful use of various artistic mediums to explore and respond to the complexities of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la)    
* Gain increased confidence in their ability to contribute to positive change and advocate for sustainable development and innovation within their communities and the wider world using artistic means. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la) 

*Action*

* Identify age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote the principles and targets of Global Goal 9 within their schools, communities, and beyond, considering both individual choices and collective advocacy. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creative skills and artistic voices to raise awareness, advocate for sustainable development, and inspire innovation that benefits all. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)    
* Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a more sustainable and innovative future for all, fostering a sense of sustained engagement and responsible global citizenship. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

Guiding Questions:  
	What do you notice about the artworks?  
	What messages or ideas do you think they are trying to convey?  
	How do these artworks make you feel?  
Introducing Street Art: Facilitate a discussion about the characteristics of street art.  
Key Points:

* Often created in public spaces for public audiences.  
* Can be used to express social and political messages.  
* Often challenges traditional art forms and institutions.  
* Can be temporary or permanent.  
* Encompasses a variety of styles and techniques (murals, stencils, graffiti, installations).

Guiding Questions:  
	What are some of the benefits and challenges of creating art in public spaces?  
	Why do you think street artists choose to express themselves in this way?  
	How can street art be used to make a difference in the world?  
Introduce or reintroduce Global Goal 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure. Explain the core idea of building resilient infrastructure, promoting inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and fostering innovation.  
Briefly introduce the different targets, using the handout to provide a visual overview.  
Guiding Questions:  
	What are some of the challenges in achieving these targets?  
	How might these targets affect different people and communities around the world?  
**Exploring Street Art Techniques**  
*Murals*  
Show examples of different mural styles and techniques.  
	Realistic Murals: Photorealistic murals that depict people, places, or objects with lifelike accuracy.  
		[“Girl With a Pierced Eardrum”,](https://banksyexplained.com/girl-with-a-pierced-eardrum-2014-3/) Banksy  
		[“The Kiss (O Beijo)”](https://www.flickr.com/photos/richard_burger/16020179392), Eduardo Kobra  
	Abstract Murals: Murals that use non-representational forms, colors, and patterns to evoke emotions and/or ideas.  
		[“Untitled”](https://www.brooklynstreetart.com/theblog/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/brooklyn-street-art-maya-hayuk_vincentLanzolla_points-de-vue-bayonne-france-10-23-web-4.jpg), Maya Hayuk  
		[“Tuttomondo”,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuttomondo) Keith Haring  
	Geometric Murals: Murals that use geometric shapes and patterns to create dynamic and visually engaging compositions.  
		[“11 Mirages to Freedom”](https://www.thisiscolossal.com/2016/03/okuda-san-miguel-wraps-a-moroccan-church-in-a-vibrant-geometric-mural/), Okuda San Miguel  
		[Multiple Murals](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=c2c3ea615d0b866b&rlz=1C5CHFA_enUS1124US1124&sxsrf=ADLYWILTbRXkCVE6barmRfObDeGMGirGlg:1733179118804&q=%22Mural%22+by+1010&udm=2&fbs=AEQNm0Aa4sjWe7Rqy32pFwRj0UkWd8nbOJfsBGGB5IQQO6L3J03RPjGV0MznOJ6Likin94pT_oR1DTSof42bOBxoTNxGUBPtuVdJiiZkfXU2rpWZHjawOPHK_JnmFlEe9Dw7kf4nGlJghtd1QWaJUyXA6x0bUFwr9aQ_HTO7YnsuGkBYkaMviQDfi37si3UNN3tyAVwbtSdEZfN5ll47y-pCe4qLj4BL7g&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiVk9Wtk4qKAxVk78kDHTidEe8QtKgLegQIGhAB&biw=1200&bih=728&dpr=2), 1010  
Discuss the use of color, composition, and perspective in murals.  
	Color: Using color to create mood, contrast, and visual interest.  
	Composition: Arranging elements within the mural to create balance, focal points, and visual flow.  
	Perspective: Using perspective techniques to create depth and dimension, making the mural appear three dimensional.  
Guiding Questions:  
	What are some of the challenges of creating large-scale artwork in public spaces?  
	How can murals be used to beautify communities and inspire social change?  
*Stencils*  
Demonstrate and/or discuss the process of creating and using stencils.  
Show examples of stencil art with different levels of complexity and detail.  
	Simple stencils with bold shapes and single colors.  
	Complex stencils with intricate details and multiple layers.  
Discuss the advantages of using stencils for creating bold and impactful images.  
	Precision: Stencils allow for clean lines and sharp edges.  
	Repetition: Stencils can be used to create repeating patterns or to reproduce the same image multiple times.  
	Efficiency: Stencils can be a quick and efficient way to create large scale artwork.  
Guiding Questions:  
	How can stencils be used to create repeating patterns or convey a specific message?  
	What are some of the creative possibilities of using stencils with different colors and layers?  
*Graffiti Art:*   
Discuss the history and evolution of graffiti art, from its origins in urban subcultures to its recognition as a legitimate art form.  
	Early graffiti: Ancient cave paintings, inscriptions on walls in ancient cities.  
	Modern graffiti: Emergence in the 1960s and 70s in Philadelphia and NYC.  
	Hip-hop culture: Connection to hip-hop music, breakdancing, and DJing  
	Street art movement: Evolution into a broader street art movement encompassing murals, stencils, etc.  
Show examples of different graffiti styles.  
	Bubble letters: Rounded, cartoon like letters  
	Wildstyle: Intricate, interlocking letters with dynamic shapes and three-dimensional effects.  
	Calligraphy: Elegant and stylized lettering inspired by calligraphy traditions.  
Discuss the use of line, color, and form in graffiti art.  
	Line: Using lines to create letterforms, shapes, and dynamic movement.  
	Color: Using color to create contrast, depth, and visual impact.  
	Form: Creating three-dimensional effects and illusions of depth through shading and perspective techniques.  
Guiding Questions:  
	What are some of the controversies surrounding graffiti art?  
	How can graffiti art be used to express individuality and challenge social norms?  
**Street Art and Global Goal 9**  
Facilitate a brainstorming session where students generate ideas for how street art can be used to represent Global Goal 9 and/or some of the targets within Global Goal 9\.  
Guiding Questions:  
	What are some visual metaphors or symbols that could represent Global Goal 9 and/or its targets?  
	How can we use street art techniques to convey the challenges and opportunities associated with Global Goal 9 and/or each target?  
	How can we use street art to inspire action and encourage people to contribute to achieving Global Goal 9?  
Examples:

* **Target 9.1** *(Resilient Infrastructure)*: Create a mural depicting a futuristic city with sustainable transportation systems and green buildings.  
* **Target 9.2** *(Inclusive and Sustainable Industrialization)*: Design a stencil series showing diverse individuals working in various industries, emphasizing the importance of inclusivity and equal opportunities.  
* **Target 9.3** *(Access to Financial Services)*: Create a graffiti artwork with symbols representing financial empowerment and access to resources for small businesses.  
* **Target 9.4** *(Sustainable Infrastructure)*: Develop an installation using recycled materials to raise awareness about the importance of resource efficiency and waste reduction.  
* **Target 9.5** *(Innovation)*: Create a mural depicting a timeline of technological advancements, highlighting the importance of scientific research and development.  
* **Target 9.a** *(Infrastructure Development in Developing Countries)*: Create a mural depicting a bridge connecting two communities, symbolizing the importance of infrastructure for connecting people and facilitating economic development.  
* **Target 9.b** *(Domestic Technology Development)*: Design a series of stencils showcasing local inventions and innovations, highlighting the creativity and ingenuity within the community.  
* **Target 9.c** *(Access to Information and Communications Technology)*: Create a graffiti artwork with symbols representing connectivity, communication, and access to information, emphasizing the importance of digital inclusion.

Divide students into smaller groups or allow them to work individually. Each group/student selects one target from GG9 (or can focus on the Goal holistically) and creates a street art-inspired piece that represents a specific aspect of that target/goal.  
Encourage students to experiment with different street art techniques to convey their chosen message effectively.  
While students work, circulate to observe, offer encouragement, and answer questions. Prompt deeper thinking:  
	I see you’re using a lot of bright colors. How does that connect to the target you chose?  
	What message are you trying to convey with this image?  
	How does your artwork encourage viewers to think about GG 9?  
**Gallery Walk and Discussion**  
Once the artwork is completed, have students display their creations around the classroom. Allow time for students to walk around, view each other’s work, and engage in informal discussions.  
Facilitate a class discussion about the artwork, focusing on the connection to GG 9 and the effectiveness of the chosen street art techniques.  
Guiding Questions:  
	What are some of the most powerful or impactful artworks you saw?  
	How did the different artworks make you think differently about GG 9?  
	What are some of the common themes or messages you noticed in the artwork?  
	How did the use of street art techniques enhance the message of the artwork?

**Reflection and Summary**  
Have students reflect on their learning experience through discussion or writing.  
Ask guiding questions like:

* How did creating street art-inspired pieces help you visualize the importance of resilient infrastructure, inclusive industrialization, and innovation for a sustainable future?  
* What new insights did you gain about the challenges and opportunities associated with Global Goal 9 through this artistic exploration?  
* Did exploring different street art techniques (murals, stencils, graffiti) change your perspective on how art can communicate complex ideas and make a difference in public spaces?  
* How did the process of creating a visually impactful piece connect with your understanding of the urgency and scale of achieving Global Goal 9?  
* What message about industry, innovation, and infrastructure do you hope your artwork would convey to someone seeing it in a public space?  
* What is one specific action you feel inspired to take after this lesson to contribute to building  more sustainable and innovate communities?

**Assessment**

* Consistent and enthusiastic involvement in all phases of the lesson, including brainstorming, art creation, group discussions, and the gallery walk.  
* Comprehension of Global Goal 9 and its core concepts, as demonstrated through the symbolism and message within their artwork and their verbal explanations.  
* Originality and effectiveness in using chosen street art elements (e.g., stencil, mural style, graffiti elements, collage) to convey their message. Look for bold visuals and impactful compositions.  
* How clearly and powerfully the finished artwork communicates its intended message about industry, innovation, and infrastructure, particularly in relation to challenges and solutions.  
* Ability to articulate artistic choices and to interpret and provide thoughtful feedback on classmates’ artwork, connecting it to Global Goal 9\.  
* Nuance and depth of understanding demonstrated in reflections, particularly concerning the role of art in social change and the complexities of achieving sustainable development.
