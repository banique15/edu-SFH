## **Objectives**

**In this curriculum, students will:**  
*Knowledge*

* Develop a comprehensive understanding of Global Goal 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure) and its critical importance for achieving sustainable development and improving human wellbeing globally. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)   
* Gain awareness of the fundamental interconnectedness between robust and equitable infrastructure, inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and the fostering of innovation as drivers of progress. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Explore the specific targets within Global Goal 9, including the development of quality,  resilient infrastructure, promoting sustainable and inclusive industrial practices, enhancing scientific research, and increasing access to technology and the internet. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la)   
* Learn about the diverse challenges and opportunities associated with achieving Global Goal 9 in different communities and countries, recognizing varying levels of development and capacity. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop foundational knowledge of various creative disciplines, including drama, visual art, creative writing, dance, and music, and their potential as powerful tools for exploring and communicating complex themes related to development and progress. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la) 

*Discourse*

* Analyze and interpret diverse forms of creative expression (drama, visual art, poetry, music, dance.) to identify and critically examine underlying themes, powerful messages, and varied perspectives related to infrastructure, industrialization, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)   
* Engage thoughtfully in nuanced discussions about complex issues related to infrastructure development, sustainable industrialization, technological innovation, and their societal and environmental impacts, demonstrating respect for diverse viewpoints.(NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Articulate their own devleoping ideas, insightful perspectives, and critical reflections on the challenges and opportunities within Global Goal 9 through both clear verbal and impactful nonverbal communication. (NCCAS: Pr5.1.la)   
* Collaborate effectively and creatively with peers to generate, develop, and share meaningful artistic projects that explore and respond to the multifaceted themes of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la) 

*Attitudes*

* Cultivate a strong sense of responsibility for promoting sustainable development and contributing to a more equitable, resilient, and prosperous future where infrastructure, industry, and innovation benefit all. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop a deepened appreciation for the transformative role of resilient infrastructure, sustainable industry, and continuous innovation in improving people's lives, fostering economic progress, and protecting the planet. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la)   
* Foster a robust sense of global citizenship and a personal commitment to taking informed and creative action to achieve the Global Goals, recognizing the interconnectedness of global challenges and solutions. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 

*Capacity*

* Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, problem-solving, and articulate communication skills through active and engaged participation in a variety of creative activities and collaborative projects focused on Global Goal 9 themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)    
* Develop their analytical abilities to interpret information and diverse perspectives from various sources, including dramatic scenes, visual artwork, movement sequences, musical compositions, creative writing, and real-world examples related to infrastructure, industry, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la)    
* Strengthen their capacity for meaningful self-expression and impactful creative communication through the thoughtful and purposeful use of various artistic mediums to explore and respond to the complexities of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la)    
* Gain increased confidence in their ability to contribute to positive change and advocate for sustainable development and innovation within their communities and the wider world using artistic means. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la) 

*Action*

* Identify age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote the principles and targets of Global Goal 9 within their schools, communities, and beyond, considering both individual choices and collective advocacy. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creative skills and artistic voices to raise awareness, advocate for sustainable development, and inspire innovation that benefits all. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)    
* Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a more sustainable and innovative future for all, fostering a sense of sustained engagement and responsible global citizenship. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 

## **Sounds from a Smarter World: Composing a World of Innovation**  
**(30-60 MINUTES)**  
**Materials**  
Music classroom or a space with ample room for movement and sound exploration  
Access to a variety of instruments (percussion, keyboards, string instruments, etc.)  
	Consider using readily available classroom objects as instruments (e.g., desks, charis, books, pencils, etc.) to create unique sounds  
Audio  
Whiteboard or large chart paper  
Markers  
Notebooks or paper  
Pens/Pencils

**Introduction**  
Begin by engaging students in a sound exploration activity. Have students close their eyes and listen to a variety of sounds (e.g., nature sounds, city sounds, machine sounds, musical excerpts).  
	Examples

* [Forest Stream Sounds, Gentle Bird Song, Relaxing Nature Sounds](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1wn-OSiNVjE)  
* [Night Walk in New York Times Square](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dq6wyZyUq1k)  
* [Sounds of a Factory](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VjD2k9A-zRs)  
* [Beethoven \- Symphony No. 5 (Proms 2012\)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jv2WJMVPQi8)

	Guiding Questions

* What sounds did you hear?  
* How did these sounds make you feel?  
* What images or ideas came to mind as you listened?

Facilitate a discussion about the connections between music and the themes of industry, innovation, and infrastructure.  
	Key Points

* Music can be used to express the rhythms and sounds of industry and technology.  
* Music can evoke emotions and ideas related to progress, innovation, and sustainability.  
* Music can inspire action and promote collaboration towards achieving Global Goal 9\.

	Guiding Questions

* How can music be used to represent the sounds of construction, manufacturing, or technology?  
* How can music convey the emotions of hope, progress, or concern related to sustainable development?  
* What are some examples of songs that inspire you to take action or make a difference in the world?

Introduce/re-introduce Global Goal 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure. Explain the core idea of building resilient infrastructure, promoting inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and fostering innovation.  
Briefly introduce the different targets within Global Goal 9, highlighting their interconnectedness and importance.  
	Guiding Questions

* What are some of the challenges in achieving these targets?  
* How might these targets affect different people and communities around the world?

**Exploring Musical Elements**  
*Rhythm:* Discuss how rhythm in music can represent the patterns and processes of industry and technology.  
	Examples

* Play recordings of music with different rhythms (e.g., a steady beat representing a factory assembly line, a syncopated rhythm representing technololgical innovation).  
  * [Kraftwerk, “The Model”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GEnx9xS79Lc)  
    * This electronic music track features a repetitive, driving beat that evokes the rhythmic precision of a factory.  
  * [Daft Punk, “Technologic”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D8K90hX4PrE)  
    * This electronic music track uses a syncopated rhythm and robotic vocals to create a sense of futuristic technology and innovation.

Have students create their own rhythmic patterns using body percussion or classroom instruments. Guide students through this if needed.  
	Guiding Questions

* How can different rhythms evoke different feelings or images related to industry and technology?  
* How can rhythm be used to create a sense of order, chaos, or progress?

*Melody:* Discuss how melody in music can convey emotions and ideas related to sustainable development.  
	Examples

* Play recordings of music with different melodies (e.g., a soaring melody representing hope and progress, a melancholic melody representing environmental concerns).  
  * [John Williams, “Theme from E.T.”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ps1-YlB1TIQ)  
    * This iconic film score features a soaring and uplifting melody that evokes a sense of wonder, hope, and possibility.  
  * [Claude Debussy, “Clair de Lune”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c977QdbTImU)  
    * This impressionist piano piece has a melancholic and reflective melody that can evoke a sense of longing or sadness, potentially connecting to conerns about environmental degredation.  
* Have students experiment with creating their own melodies using their instruments or their own voices. Guide students through this if needed.

	Guiding Questions

* How can different melodies evoke different emotions or moods related to sustainability?  
* How can melody be used to tell a story or express a message about the environment or social justice?

*Harmony:* Discuss how harmony in music can represent collaboration and interconnectedness, essential for achieving Global Goal 9\.  
	Examples

* Play recordings of music with different harmonies (e.g., consonant harmonies representing unity and cooperation, dissonant harmonies representing conflict or challenges).  
  * [The Staple Singers, “I’ll Take You There”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IhHBr7nMMio)  
    * This classic pop song features beautiful vocal harmonies that create a sense of unity, peace, and cooperation.  
  * [Igor Stravinsky, “The Rite of Spring (Sacrificial Dance)”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BiH3vA7q0jo)  
    * This avant-garde ballet score features dissonant and jarring harmonies that create a sense of tension, conflict, and chaos.  
* Have students experiment with creating simple harmonies using their instruments or their own voices. Guide students through this if needed.

	Guiding Questions

* How can harmony be used to create a sense of balance, tension, or resolution in music?  
* How can harmony represent the collaboration needed to achieve the Global Goals, and in particular Global Goal 9?

*Dynamics:* Discuss how dynamics (loudness and softness) in music can represent the power and impact of industry and innovation.  
	Examples

* Play recordings of music with varying dynamics (e.g., loud and powerful sections representing industrial processes, quiet and reflective sections representing the impact on communities or the environment).  
  * [Gustav Holst, “The Planets, Op. 32: IV. Jupiter, the Bringer of Jollity”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IBLghXA8NCk)  
    * This orchestral piece features a wide range of dynamics, from quiet and reflective passages to loud and triumphant sections, which can be used to represent the varying power and impact of industrial processes and their effects on communities and the environment.  
  * [Radiohead, “Paranoid Android”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YTH8cxXBGBo)  
    * This progressive rock song is known for its dramatic shifts in dynamics and mood, which can be interpreted as a reflection of the unpredictable nature of technological advancement and its impact on society.  
* Have students experiment with creating dynamic variations in their own music-making. Guide students through this if needed.

	Guiding Questions

* How can dynamics be used to create contrast, emphasis, or emotional impact in music?  
* How can dynamics represent the scale and impact of industrial development or technological advancements?

**Music and Global Goal 9**  
Explore how the musical elements introduced can be put into practice to represent the themes and targets of Global Goal 9 through a guided activity.  
*Rhythm*  
Different rhythms can represent various aspects of industry, innovation, and infrastructure.  
	Example

* A steady, repetitive rhythm could represent the consistent work of a factory, while a more syncopated or irregular rhythm could represent technological innovation or the construction of new infrastructure.

Lead students in a call-and-response activity where you create a rhythm using body percussion (clapping, snapping, stomping, etc.), and students echo the rhythm back to you.  
Gradually increase the complexity of rhythms, incorporating different dynamics and tempos to represent different aspects of Global Goal 9\.  
	Example

* Create a rhythm that starts slow and simple, then gradually becomes faster and more complex, representing the progress and development of industry and technology.

*Melody*  
Different melodies can evoke emotions and ideas related to Global Goal 9\.  
	Example

* A soaring, uplifting melody could represent the hope and optimism associated with sustainable development, while a more melancholic melody could represent the challenges and concerns related to environmental sustainability.

Lead students in a vocal exploration where you sing a simple melody or phrase, and students echo it back to you, experimenting with different dynamics and expressions.  
	Example

* Create a melody that starts with a single note, gradually adding more notes and harmonies to represent the growth and interconnectedness of sustainable industries.

*Harmony*  
Harmony in music can represent collaboration and interconnectedness, essential for achieving Global Goal 9\.  
	Example

* Have students sing or play simple chords or harmonies together, emphasizing the importance of each part contributing to the overall sound.

Connect this to Target 9.2 (Inclusive Industrialization) by discussing the importance of diverse skills and talents contributing to a thriving workforce.  
*Dynamics*  
Dynamics in music can represent the power and impact of industry and innovation.  
	Example

* Have students experiment with different dynamics while singing or playing instruments, creating contrasts and variations that represent the different aspects of Global Goal 9\.

Connect this to Target 9.4 (Sustainable Infrastructure) by discussing how industries can reduce their environmental impact and promote sustainable practices.  
*Sound Effects*  
Sound effects can be used to represent different aspects of industry, innovation, and infrastructure.  
	Example

* Have students create sound effects using their voices, body percussion, or classroom objects to represent the sounds of construction, transportation, or technology.

Connect this to Target 9.5 (Innovation) by discussing how new technologies and inventions can create new sounds and possibilities.  
**Sharing and Discussion**  
Have students share their experiences with the guided music activity. Encourage them to describe the sounds they created, the emotions they felt, and the connections they made to Global Goal 9\.  
	Guided Questions

* How did the different musical elements (rhythm, melody, harmony, dynamics) help you understand the themes of Global Goal 9?  
* What were some of the most effective or memorable sounds or musical moments?  
* How did the activity make you think differently about the role of industry, innovation, and infrastructure in creating a sustainable future?  
* How can music and sound be used to raise awareness and inspire action on global issues?


  
**Reflection and Summary**  
Have students reflect on their learning experience through discussion or writing.  
Ask guiding questions like:

* How did creating and exploring sounds \- using rhythm, melody, harmony, and dynamics \- help you to understand the themes of industry, innovation, and infrastructure in a new way?  
* What did you learn about how different musical elements can represent specific aspects of Global Goal 9, such as the consistent work of a factory, the sudden burst of innovation, or the complexity of interconnected infrastructure?  
* Did the activity change how you perceive the sounds around you in your daily life? Do you now hear “music” or patterns in everyday industry and technology?  
* How did actively making music, rather than just listening, deepen your personal connection to Global Goal 9?  
* What did you learn about the power of music and sound to evoke emotions and communicate messages about progress, challenges, and sustainability?  
* How did exploring Global Goal 9 through music and sound help you to grasp their interconnectedness?  
* Did any particular sound or musical moment created by the class powerfully represent a challenge or solution related to Global Goal 9?  
* What role do you think innovation and technology play in achieving sustainability, as reflected in the sounds we explored?  
* How can understanding “sounds of progress” motivate us to contribute to building a more sustainable future?  
* Did this lesson spark any new ideas about how you might use music or sound in your own life to advocate for Global Goal 9?  
* What is one key takeaway about industry, innovation, and infrastructure that you will remember from this musical exploration?  
* How has this lesson influenced your thinking about your own role in fostering innovation or supporting sustainable infrastructure in your community?  
* What kind of impact do you think a powerful soundscape or musical piece about Global Goal 9 could have on listeners?  
* How can music truly inspire change and promote collaboration towards achieving the Global Goals?

**Assessment**

* Consistent and enthusiastic involvement in all phases of the lesson, including sound explorations, rhythmic exercises, guided music activities, and class discussions.  
* Comprehension of Global Goal 9 and its connection to industry, innovation, and infrastructure, as demonstrated through musical choices (e.g., specific rhythms, melodies, dynamics used) and verbal explanations.  
* Originality and effectiveness in using chosen musical elements (rhythm, melody, harmony, dynamics, sound effects) to convey their message about Global Goal 9\. Look for innovative use of available instruments or sounds.  
* How clearly and powerfully the collective musical exploration communicated its intended message about Global Goal 9\. Did the soundscape evoke thought or emotion related to the Goal?  
* Ability to work effectively as a class, listening to each other’s contributions, responding musically, and contributing to the cohesive overall sound.  
* Nuance and insight demonstrated in reflections, particularly concerning the role of sound and music in understanding global challenges and the complexities of achieving sustainable development.
