## **Musical Exploration** 

**(10-30 MINUTES) (GRADES 5-12)**  
**Materials**  
	Audio  
Lyrics Sheets (optional and dependent on the song chosen)  
**Song Examples**	  
Brave by Sara Bareilles  
Fight Song by Rachel Platten  
Not Afraid by Eminem  
Heal the World by Michael Jackson  
Breathe Me, Sia  
Demons, Imagine Dragons  
Streets of Philadelphia, Bruce Springsteen  
Everybody Hurts, R.E.M.  
In My Blood, Shawn Mendes  
Breathin', Ariana Grande  
**Discussion**

* Ask students to reflect on the lyrics, melody, and overall mood of the song(s)  
* What emotions do you feel when you hear this song?  
* What messages about health and well-being does the song convey?  
* How does the artist's background or experiences influence their perspective on these issues?  
* Can you relate the song's message to any real-life situations or challenges related to health?  
* How does this song connect to specific targets of Global Goal 3 (e.g., ending preventable diseases, promoting mental health, etc.)?  
* Emphasize how music can be a powerful tool for expressing emotions, raising awareness, and inspiring action around health and well-being.

**Personal Connection**

* Ask students if any of the lyrics or themes resonate with their own experiences or observations about health and well-being.  
* Have they ever felt similar challenges or witnessed situations that connect to the song's message?  
* Encourage students to share their personal stories, while respecting privacy and creating a safe space for open discussion.

**Closing Activity Options**  
	**One Word Summary:** Ask each student to think of one word encapsulating their biggest takeaway or feeling about poverty after this musical exploration. Have them share their word with the class, briefly explaining their choice. **Bonus:** Collect all the words to make a word cloud.  
	**Quote Connection:** Share an inspiring quote about health and well-being. Facilitate an open discussion about how this quote connects to the music and activity they experienced in class. **Example:** "The health of the people is really the foundation upon which all their happiness and all their powers as a state depend." \- Benjamin Disraeli  
	**Songwriting Challenge:** Divide students into small groups and challenge them to write a short song or rap verse about health and well-being. Encourage them to consider the challenges, solutions, and the importance of achieving health and well-being.  
	**Global Goal Playlist:** Have students work in groups to create a playlist of songs that represent different aspects of Global Goal 3\.
