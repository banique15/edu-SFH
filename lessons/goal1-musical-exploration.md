## **MUSICAL EXPLORATION** 

**(10-30 MINUTES) (GRADES 5-12)**  
**Materials**  
	Audio  
Lyrics Sheets (optional and dependent on the song chosen)  
**Song Examples**	  
9 to 5, Dolly Parton  
A Change is Gonna Come by Sam Cooke (soul, hope)  
	Glory by Common and John Legend (hip hop, social justice)  
	We Are the World by USA for Africa (pop, charity)  
Streets of London by Ralph McTell  
Another Day in Paradise by Phil Collins  
Fast Car by Tracy Chapman  
Hard Times by Paramore  
Hard Times Come Again No More by Stephen Foster  
La Tierra es de Quien la Trabaja (This Land Belongs to Those Who Work It) by Grupo Aymara  
Soweto Blues by Miriam Makeba  
Water No Get Enemy by Fela Kuti  
Ya Rayah (Those Who Emigrate) by Rachid Taha  
Pobre corazón (Poor Heart) by Divididos  
**Ojalá Que Llueva Café by Juan Luis Guerra**  
**Discussion**

* Ask students to reflect on the lyrics, melody, and overall mood of the song(s)  
* What emotions do you feel when you hear this song?  
* What messages about poverty does the song convey?  
* How does the artist's background or experiences influence their perspective on poverty?  
* Can you relate the song's message to any real-life situations or issues?  
* Emphasize how music can be a powerful tool for expressing emotions, raising awareness, and inspiring action around social issues.

**Personal Connection**

* Ask students if any of the lyrics or themes resonate with their own experiences or observations about poverty.  
* Have they ever felt similar emotions or witnessed situations that connect to the song's message?

**Closing Activity Options**  
	**One Word Summary:** Ask each student to think of one word encapsulating their biggest takeaway or feeling about poverty after this musical exploration. Have them share their word with the class, briefly explaining their choice. **Bonus:** Collect all the words to make a word cloud.  
	**Quote Connection:** Share an inspiring quote about poverty or resilience. Facilitate an open discussion about how this quote connects to the music and activity they experienced in class. **Example:** "Poverty is not an accident. Like slavery and apartheid, it is man-made and can be removed by the actions of human beings." Nelson Mandela  
	**Songwriting Challenge:** Divide students into small groups and challenge them to write a short song or rap verse about poverty and/or resilience. Encourage them to consider the challenges, solutions, and the importance of achieving No Poverty.
