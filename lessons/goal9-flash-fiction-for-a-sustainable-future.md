## **Objectives**

**In this curriculum, students will:**  
*Knowledge*

* Develop a comprehensive understanding of Global Goal 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure) and its critical importance for achieving sustainable development and improving human wellbeing globally. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)   
* Gain awareness of the fundamental interconnectedness between robust and equitable infrastructure, inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and the fostering of innovation as drivers of progress. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Explore the specific targets within Global Goal 9, including the development of quality,  resilient infrastructure, promoting sustainable and inclusive industrial practices, enhancing scientific research, and increasing access to technology and the internet. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la)   
* Learn about the diverse challenges and opportunities associated with achieving Global Goal 9 in different communities and countries, recognizing varying levels of development and capacity. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop foundational knowledge of various creative disciplines, including drama, visual art, creative writing, dance, and music, and their potential as powerful tools for exploring and communicating complex themes related to development and progress. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la) 

*Discourse*

* Analyze and interpret diverse forms of creative expression (drama, visual art, poetry, music, dance.) to identify and critically examine underlying themes, powerful messages, and varied perspectives related to infrastructure, industrialization, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)   
* Engage thoughtfully in nuanced discussions about complex issues related to infrastructure development, sustainable industrialization, technological innovation, and their societal and environmental impacts, demonstrating respect for diverse viewpoints.(NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)   
* Articulate their own devleoping ideas, insightful perspectives, and critical reflections on the challenges and opportunities within Global Goal 9 through both clear verbal and impactful nonverbal communication. (NCCAS: Pr5.1.la)   
* Collaborate effectively and creatively with peers to generate, develop, and share meaningful artistic projects that explore and respond to the multifaceted themes of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la) 

*Attitudes*

* Cultivate a strong sense of responsibility for promoting sustainable development and contributing to a more equitable, resilient, and prosperous future where infrastructure, industry, and innovation benefit all. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)    
* Develop a deepened appreciation for the transformative role of resilient infrastructure, sustainable industry, and continuous innovation in improving people's lives, fostering economic progress, and protecting the planet. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la)   
* Foster a robust sense of global citizenship and a personal commitment to taking informed and creative action to achieve the Global Goals, recognizing the interconnectedness of global challenges and solutions. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 

*Capacity*

* Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, problem-solving, and articulate communication skills through active and engaged participation in a variety of creative activities and collaborative projects focused on Global Goal 9 themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)    
* Develop their analytical abilities to interpret information and diverse perspectives from various sources, including dramatic scenes, visual artwork, movement sequences, musical compositions, creative writing, and real-world examples related to infrastructure, industry, and innovation. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la)    
* Strengthen their capacity for meaningful self-expression and impactful creative communication through the thoughtful and purposeful use of various artistic mediums to explore and respond to the complexities of Global Goal 9\. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la)    
* Gain increased confidence in their ability to contribute to positive change and advocate for sustainable development and innovation within their communities and the wider world using artistic means. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la) 

*Action*

* Identify age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote the principles and targets of Global Goal 9 within their schools, communities, and beyond, considering both individual choices and collective advocacy. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)    
* Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creative skills and artistic voices to raise awareness, advocate for sustainable development, and inspire innovation that benefits all. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)    
* Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a more sustainable and innovative future for all, fostering a sense of sustained engagement and responsible global citizenship. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 

## **Flash Fiction for a Sustainable Future**  
**(30-60 MINUTES)**  
**Materials:**  
Paper or notebooks  
Pens or pencils  
Whiteboard or large chart paper  
Markers  
Images or videos related to Global Goal 9  
Examples of Flash Fiction (see examples)

	What challenges or opportunities do you see?  
	How does this relate to the idea of building a sustainable future?  
Introduce Flash Fiction  
	Facilitate a discussion about the characteristics of flash fiction.  
	Key Points

* Very short stories, typically under 1,000 words.  
* Focus on a single moment, event, or idea.  
* Use concise and impactful language.  
* Often have a surprising twist or unexpected theme.  
* Can explore a wide range of themes and emotions.

	Guiding Questions

* What are some of the benefits and challenges of writing very short stories?  
* How can flash fiction be used to convey powerful messages or ideas?  
* What are some examples of flash fiction stories you have read or heard?

Introduce/re-introduce Global Goal 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure. Explain the core idea of building resilient infrastructure, promoting inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and fostering innovation.  
Briefly introduce the different targets within GG 9, highlighting their interconnectedness and importance, using the handout with GG9 information to provide a visual overview of the targets.  
	Guiding Questions

* What are some of the challenges in achieving these targets?  
* How might these targets affect different people and communities around the world?

**Exploring Flash Fiction Elements**  
*Brevity:* Discuss the importance of concise language and choosing every word carefully in flash fiction.  
	Examples

* “For sale: baby shoes, never worn.”    
  * This classic six-word story misattributed to  Ernest Hemingway demonstrates the power of brevity and implication. [More information on this story.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/For_sale:_baby_shoes,_never_worn)  
* “The Last Man on Earth sat alone in a room. There was a knock on the door.” \- By Fredric Brown  
  * This is a fantastic example of a surprising twist or turning point. It sets up one idea (loneliness) then completely flips it with the unexpected knock, leaving the readers with a powerful question.  
* “The world ended. Luckily, my pet rock survived.”  
  * This is a modern, often shared example from online flash fiction communities. It’s funny but also shows how even a tiny story can have a problem and solution or a unique perspective.  
* “A girl woke up. Her legs were roots. Her arms, branches. She smiled. Sunlight warmed her leaves.”  
  * This type of transformative flash fiction is common. It uses strong imagery and a clear sense of change within a very short space.

	Guiding Questions

* How can you make every word count in your writing?  
* What are some strategies for creating a strong impact with limited space?

*Impactful Language:* Discuss the use of strong verbs, vivid imagery, and sensory details to create engaging flash fiction.  
	Examples

* Analyze the use of figurative language (metaphors, similes, personification) in flash fiction examples.  
* Discuss how authors create a sense of atmosphere and modd through their word choice.  
* From “The Lottery” by Shirley Jackson: “The morning of June 27th was clear and sunny, with the fresh warmth of a full-summer day; the flowers were blossoming profusely and the grass was richly green.” \- This sets a deceptively idyllic scene before the story’s dark turn. [More information on “The Lottery.”](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lottery)

	Guiding Questions

* How can you use language to create a specific mood or tone in your story?  
* What are some ways to create vivid imagery and sensory experiences for the reader?

*Surprising Twists:* Discuss the element of surprise and unexpected endings in flash fiction.  
	Examples

* Share examples of flash fiction stories with surprising twists or thought-provoking endings.  
* Analyze how authors use foreshadowing, irony, or ambiguity to create unexpected turns in their narratives.  
* “Harrison Bergeron” by Kurt Vonnegut \- This dystopian story uses satire and a shocking ending to comment on forced equality. More information on [“Harrison Bergeron.”](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harrison_Bergeron)

	Guiding Questions

* How can you create a sense of suspense or anticipation in your story?  
* What are some ways to surprise the reader with an unexpected ending or revelation?

**Flash Fiction and Global Goal 9**  
Facilitate a brainstorming session where students generate ideas for flash fiction stories that connect to the different targets within Global Goal 9\.  
	Guiding Questions

* What are some potential conflicts or challenges that could be explored in a story about sustainable development?  
* How can we use flash fiction to highlight the human impact of infrastructure, industrialization, and innovation?  
* What are some creative ways to incorporate the targets of Global Goal 9 into a short story?

	Examples

* **Target 9.1** *(Resilient Infrastructure):* A story about a community rebuilding after a natural disaster, highlighting the importance of resilient infrastructure for disaster preparedness and recovery. *Possible twist:* The new infrastructure is even more vulnerable than what it replaced.  
* **Target 9.2** *(Inclusive and Sustainable Industrialization):* A story about a young entrepreneur starting a business that uses sustainable practices and creates opportunities for marginalized communities. *Possible twist:* The entrepreneur faces unexpected opposition from within the very community they aim to help.  
* **Target 9.3** *(Access to Financial Services):* A story about a small business owner in a developing country who gains access to financial services and is able to expand their business and create jobs. *Possible twist:* The business owner discovers hidden conditions in the loan agreement that threaten their livelihood.  
* **Target 9.4** *(Sustainable Infrastructure):* A story about a city that transforms its infrastructure to become more environmentally friendly, using renewable energy and reducing waste. *Possible twist:* The “green” solutions have unintended negative consequences for a segment of the population.  
* **Target 9.5** *(Innovation):* A story about a scientist who develops a groundbreaking technology that helps to solve a global challenge. *Possible twist:* The technology has unforeseen negative side effects or falls into the wrong hands.  
* **Target 9.a** *(Infrastructure Development in Developing Countries):* A story about a remote village that gains access to electricity and the internet, transforming their lives and opportunities. *Possible twist:* The influx of technology disrupts traditional ways of life and creates new social problems.  
* **Target 9.b** *(Domestic Technology Development):* A story about a group of students who invent a new technology to address a local environmental problem. *Possible twist:* Their invention is stolen by a large corporation.  
* **Target 9.c** *(Access to Information and Communications Technology):* A story about a student who uses technology to connect with other around the world and learn about different cultures and perspectives. *Possible twist:* The online world proves to be A source of misinformation and harmful influences.

Provide dedicated time for students to write their flash fiction stories. Encourage them to use the brainstorming ideas and elements of flash fiction discussed earlier.  
Circulate the classroom to offer support, answer questions, and provide feedback as needed.  
**Sharing and Discussion (Optional)**  
Invite students to share their flash fiction stories. This can be done through volunteer readings, small group sharing, or a gallery walk where students post their stories and read each other’s work.  
Facilitate a class discussion about the stories, focusing on the connection to Global Goal 9, the effectivenes of the writing techniques, and the overall impact of the stories.  
	Guiding Questions

* What were some of the most impactful or memorable stories you heard/read?  
* How did these stories make you think differently about GG 9?  
* What were some of the common themes or messages you noticed in the stories?  
* How did the authors use language and storytelling techniques to create engaging and thought-provoking narratives?  
* Which stories had the most surprising twists? How did those twists affect your interpretation of the story?

	**Note:** If sharing and discussion is not wanted, students can hand in their flash fiction stories for review.

**Reflection and Summary**  
Have students reflect on their learning experience through discussion or writing.  
Ask guiding questions like:

* How did writing a flash fiction story about Global Goal 9 help you understand the human impact of industry, innovation, and infrastructure?  
* What new insights did you gain about the challenges and opportunities of building a sustainable future, as explored in your story or your classmates’ stories?  
* Did using concise and impactful language (as in flash fiction) make the issues related to Global Goal 9 feel more urgent or personal? Why or why not?  
* What role do you think creative writing and storytelling can play in raising awareness and inspiring action for Global Goal 9?  
* What is one key takeaway about industry, innovation, and infrastructure that you will remember from this lesson?

**Assessment**

* Consistent and enthusiastic involvement in all phases of the lesson, including initial brainstorming, understanding flash fiction elements, the writing process, and discussions.  
* Comprehension of Global Goal 9 and any specific targets and the core concepts, as demonstrated throught the narrative and thematic content of the flash fiction story and the verbal explanations.  
* Originality and effectiveness in using flash fiction elements such as brevity, impactful language, vivid imagery, and a single focused moment or idea within the story.  
* How clearly and powerfully their finished story communicates its intended message about industry, innovation, and infrastructure, particularly in relation to challenges and potential solutions. Look for the story’s ability to evoke thought or emotion.  
* Quality of the writing craft, including word choice, sensory details, and the creation of atmosphere, especially within the limited word count.  
* Ability to articulate creative choices and to interpret and provide thoughtful feedback on classmates’ stories, connecting them to Global Goal 9 themes (if applicable).  
* Nuance and depth of understanding demonstrated in reflections, particularly concerning the role of storytelling in advocating for sustainable development.
