## Objectives

### Knowledge
- Understand the key targets and indicators of the United Nations' Global Goal 5: Gender Equality, recognizing its comprehensive scope encompassing the rights and empowerment of all women and girls. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Recognize the diverse and interconnected forms of gender inequality and discrimination that persist globally, including systemic violence, harmful practices, unequal access to resources and opportunities, and limited participation in decision-making processes. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Identify and analyze a range of potential solutions and actions at individual, community, national, and international levels that can effectively address these challenges and promote gender equality and the empowerment of all women and girls. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

### Discourse
- Analyze and interpret diverse artistic perspectives on gender equality across various art forms (creative writing, visual art, music, drama, and dance), identifying the techniques used to convey meaning, evoke empathy, and challenge societal norms. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)
- Engage thoughtfully and respectfully in critical discussions about complex gender roles, harmful stereotypes, and the fundamental importance of empowering women and girls for the betterment of individuals and society as a whole. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Express their own nuanced thoughts, feelings, and developing ideas about gender equality through purposeful and impactful creative expression in a variety of artistic mediums, including crafting compelling narratives, visual representations, musical compositions,movement activities, and dramatic performances. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr5.1.la)

### Attitudes
- Develop a deepened sense of empathy and understanding towards the lived experiences of individuals and communities affected by gender inequality, fostering a more informed and compassionate perspective on the challenges faced by women and girls globally. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Challenge their own and societal harmful gender stereotypes, biases, and assumptions, actively questioning traditional norms and promoting a more inclusive and equitable worldview that values the diverse perspectives and experiences of all genders. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Cultivate a strong sense of social responsibility and ethical awareness, fostering a personal commitment to actively advocating for gender equality and contributing to positive social change within their communities and the wider world. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
- Foster a deep respect for diversity and inclusivity, recognizing the intersectionality of gender with other social identities (such as race, ethnicity, class, sexual orientation, and disability) and understand how these intersecting identities can compound experiences of inequality. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

### Capacity
- Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, communication, and collaborative skills through engaging in a variety of interactive artistic activities, including analyzing existing works and developing their own creative responses to gender equality themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
- Develop their ability to effectively express complex emotions, articulate nuanced ideas, and convey diverse perspectives related to gender equality through the purposeful use of different artistic mediums and techniques. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
- Gain a deeper understanding of the powerful role of the arts in giving voice to marginalized groups, challenging dominant narratives, fostering empathy, and inspiring meaningful action towards achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)

### Action
- Identify and critically evaluate a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote gender equality within their school, community, and beyond, considering the complexities of systemic change and individual agency. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creativity and artistic expression as powerful tools for raising awareness about gender inequality, challenging harmful stereotypes, and advocating for positive change at various levels. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
- Develop and collaboratively implement thoughtful and ethical action plans or artistic interventions aimed at making a positive impact on gender equality issues within their communities and the wider world. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
- Reflect thoughtfully on their evolving role as engaged global citizens in creating a more just and equitable world for all genders, fostering a personal commitment to lifelong learning and active participation in the pursuit of gender equality. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

## Action Planning

Empowering youth to become active agents of change in the pursuit of achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls. Recoginzing that even small, consistent actions can contribute to a larger global impact.

### Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
- Challenge Gender Stereotypes: Be aware of and actively challenge gender stereotypes in conversations, media, and everyday situations.
- Use Inclusive Language: Practice using gender-neutral language where appropriate.
- Promote Equal Opportunities: Encourage friends of all genders to pursue their interests, talents, and career aspirations without limitation based on traditional gender roles.
- Share Responsibilities at Home: Actively participate in household chores and caregiving responsibilities, regardless of traditional gender roles. This helps to value unpaid care work.
- Speak Up Against Discrimination: If you witness any form of discrimination or unfair treatment based on gender, find a safe way to speak up or inform a trusted adult.
- Support and Celebrate All Genders: Recognize and celebrate the achievements and contributions of people of all genders in all fields, from STEM to arts and sports.
- Educate Yourself and Others: Stay informed about issues related to gender inequality, including violence against women and girls and harmful practices.
- Promote Healthy Relationships: Understand and practice consent, respect, and open communication in all relationships. This is crucial for eliminating violence and promoting reproductive rights.
- Be a Responsible Digital Citizen: Challenge online harassment, hate speech, and gender-based discrimination on social media and other digital platforms, contributing to a safer online environment.
- Value Diverse Perspectives: Actively listen to and respect the experiences and viewpoints of individuals from all genders and backgrounds.

### Holistic Actions

#### Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice)
- Utilize your artistic talents (creative writing, visual art, music, dance, drama) to develop powerful pieces that explore the themes of gender equality, challenge harmful practices, celebrate empowerment, or advocate for Global Goal 5.
- Organize and facilitate short presentations or interactive workshops during lunch breaks, club meetings, or in class to discuss various aspects of gender inequality, such as the importance of women’s leadership or the value of unpaid care work.
- Join or initiative campaigns within your school or community to raise awareness about specific gender equality issues, such as ending gender-based violence or promoting equal pay.

#### Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community)
- Research and connect with local organizations that work on issues related to gender equality, such as women’s shelters, domestic violence support services, or programs that empower girls through education or skill-building.
- Offer to volunteer with local groups that promote women’s leadership, provide safe spaces for girls, or support survivors of gender-based violence. This directly contributes to empowering women and girls in your community.
- Encourage and model shared responsibility for tasks and leadership roles in group projects, sports teams, and extracurricular activities, ensuring tha all voices are heard and valued.

#### Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
- Investigate how gender inequality manifests differently across cultures and regions, exploring the specific challenges faces by women and girls in different parts of the world related to violence, harmful practices, economic rights, and political participation.
- Learn about international conventions and agreements aimed at promoting gender equality, such as the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW) or the Beijing Platform for Action.
- Stay informed about global campaigns and movements advocating for women’s and girls’ rights, such as #HeForShe or initiatives by UN Women.
- Seek out documentaries, books, articles, or podcasts that feature the perspectives and experiences of women and girls from diverse cultural, racial, and socioeconomic backgrounds globally.
