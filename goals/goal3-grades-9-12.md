## Objectives

### Knowledge
- Identify and differentiate between the interconnected dimensions of health and well-being, encompassing physical, mental, emotional, and social aspects, and analyze how these contribute to a holistic and fulfilling life. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Recognize the complex global challenges related to health, including disparities in access to quality healthcare, the burden of communicable and non-communicable diseases, the impact of environmental factors, and the social determinants of health. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

### Discourse
- Analyze and interpret the diverse ways in which music and other art forms can express a wide range of emotions, raise critical awareness, and advocate for positive change related to varioushealth issues and wellbeing. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)
- Engage thoughtfully and  respectfully in meaningful discussions about health and wellbeing from multiple perspectives, exploring diverse experiences, cultural beliefs, and ethical considerations surrounding health. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Express their own nuanced thoughts, feelings, and developing ideas about personal and global health through purposeful and impactful creative processes in music composition, visual art, creative writing, dance, and drama. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la)

### Attitudes
- Develop a deepened sense of empathy and compassion for individuals and communities facing a wide spectrum of health challenges, fostering a more humane and understanding perspective on health disparities. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Challenge societal stereotypes and reduce stigma associated with mental health, addiction, chronic illnesses, and other health issues, promoting open dialogue and a more supportive and inclusive community. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Cultivate a strong sense of personal responsibility for their own health and wellbeing, alongside a growing awareness of their interconnectedness with the health of others and the global community. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
- Foster a sense of global citizenship and a personal desire to contribute to creating a healthier, more equitable, and sustainable world for everyone. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)

### Capacity
- Enhance their creativity, collaboration, and articulate communication skills through in-depth group projects, artistic collaborations, and the development of impactful creative responses to health-related themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
- Develop advanced critical thinking skills to thoroughly analyze the root causes of health disparities, evaluate the effectiveness and ethical implications of potential solutions, and understand the complexities of global health challenges. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Gain a deeper understanding of the unique and powerful  role of the arts in promoting health and wellbeing, raising awareness, fostering empathy, and inspiring positive action on health-related issues. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)

### Action
- Identify and critically evaluate a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to improve their own health and wellbeing across its various dimensions. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la)
- Explore and develop meaningful ways to contribute to the achievement of Global Goal 3 within their local communities and beyond, considering advocacy, awareness-raising, and direct support. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Use their chosen art form as a powerful tool for advocacy and raising public awareness about critical health issues, promoting understanding, reducing stigma, and inspiring positive change. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la)
- Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a healthier and more equitable world for all, fostering a sense of sustained engagement and responsible global citizenship. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

## Action Planning

Empowering youth to become active agents of change in the pursuit of healthy lives and promoting wellbeing for all at all ages. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can contribute to a larger global impact.

### Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
- Prioritize Personal Wellness: Make conscious choices daily that support your physical, mental, and emotional health, such as getting enough sleep, eating nutritious foods, staying physically active, and managing stress. This directly embodies the core of Global Goal 3.
- Practice Empathy and Active Listening: When interacting with others, especially those facing health challenges, listen without judgement and offer support. This contributes to reducing stigma around mental health and chronic illnesses.
- Be a Responsible Road User: Follow traffic laws, avoid distracted driving (or being a distracted pedestrian), and encourage others to do the same.
- Stay Informed About Health: Read reliable sources about common illnesses, healthy habits, and public health campaigns. Share accurate information with friends and family to promote general health literacy.
- Practice Good Hygiene: Simple actions like handwashing and proper waste disposal help prevent the spread of communicable diseases.
- Reduce Chemical Exposure: Make conscious choices about household products and personal care items to minimize exposure to hazardous chemicals.
- Conserve Water and Reduce Pollution: Be mindful of your water usage and proper disposal of waste to reduce water and soil contamination.
- Advocate for Inclusivity: Ensure that activities and spaces are accessible and welcoming to people of all ages and abilities, promoting wellbeing for all.
- Support Others in Healthy Choices: Encourage friends and family to make healthy decisions and offer a supportive environment for those seeking to improve their wellbeing.

### Holistic Actions

#### Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice)
- Utilize your artistic talents (music, visual art, creative writing, dance, drama) to develop pieces that shed light on specific health challenges, promote mental health awareness, celebrate wellbeing, or advocate for better healthcare access. Share these through school exhibitions, online platforms, or community events.
- Lead discussions or presentations in classes, during club meetings, or for community groups on topics like mental health stigma, preventable diseases, road safety, or the importance of vaccines. Use compelling stories and data to engage your audience.
- Research and engage with organizations advocating for health-related policies, such as increased funding for public health, universal healthcare, or improved access to mental health services. Participate in letter-writing campaigns or petition drives.

#### Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community)
- Identify and volunteer your time with local hospitals, clinics, senior centers, mental health support groups, or organizations that promote healthy lifestyles. This directly supports local health services.
- Plan and execute events at school or in your community focused on promoting health.
- Get involved with local initiatives that aim to improve health outcomes, such as advocating for safer pedestrian infrastructure, participating in community clean-up efforts to reduce pollution, or supporting local substance abuse prevention programs.
- Work with school administration and student councils to advocate for healthier food options in the cafeteria, improved access to school nurses or counselors, or better physical education programs.

#### Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
- Investigate health challenges and disparities in different parts of the world, focusing on why certain populations disproportionately suffer from preventable diseases, lack of healthcare access, or environmental health risks.
- Learn how factors like socioeconomic status, education, race, and gender influence health outcomes globally.
- Regularly check for news and updates from international health organizations like the World Health Organization (WHO), UNICEF, or Doctors Without Borders to stay informed about global health crises and progress towards Global Goal 3.
- Learn about new research, vaccines, medicines, and healthcare delivery models being developed to address health challenges, particularly those affecting developing countries.
