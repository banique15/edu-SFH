GRADES 9-12
Objectives
In this curriculum, students will:
Knowledge
Develop a comprehensive understanding of the concept of inequality and its various interconnected forms, including social, economic, and political disparities, both within and among countries. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Gain critical awareness of the root causes and far-reaching consequences of inequality, examining its impact on individuals, communities, and global stability. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
Learn about the United Nations Global Goal 10 and its targets, analyzing how they aim to progressively achieve income growth for the poorest, promote social and economic inclusion, eliminate discriminatory practices, and ensure equitable representation in global institutions. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la)
Understand the role of arts and creative expression in illuminating hidden inequalities, fostering empathy, and inspiring dialogue about social justice. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la)
Discourse
Engage thoughtfully and respectfully in nuanced discussions about complex issues of inequality and social justice, exploring diverse perspectives, lived experiences, and the challenges and opportunities in achieving Global Goal 10. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
Analyze and interpret various forms of creative expression (visual art, creative writing, dance, drama, and music) to identify themes, powerful messages, and varied perspectives related to inequality, discrimination, and social inclusion. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)
Articulate their own developing and informed ideas, insightful perspectives, and critical reflections on the dynamics of inequality through both clear verbal communication and impactful nonverbal artistic expression. (NCCAS: Pr5.1.la)
Collaborate effectively and creatively with peers to generate, develop, and share meaningful artistic projects that explore and respond to the multifaceted themes of Global Goal 10. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
Attitudes
Develop a deepened sense of empathy and compassion for individuals and communities facing challenges related to inequality and discrimination, fostering a more informed and understanding perspective. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
Challenge their own and societal stereotypes, biases, and discriminatory practices that perpetuate inequality, actively questioning norms and promoting a more inclusive and equitable worldview. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
Cultivate a strong sense of social responsibility and a personal commitment to actively promoting social justice and contributing to creating a more equitable world where everyone has the opportunity to thrive. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
Foster a robust sense of global citizenship and a sustained commitment to taking informed and creative action to address inequality within their communities and beyond. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
Capacity
Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, problem-solving, and articulate communication skills through active and engaged participation in a variety of creative activities and collaborative projects focused on inequality. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
Develop their analytical abilities to interpret information and diverse perspectives from various artistic sources, written texts, dance performances, dramatic skits, and musical pieces related to social and economic disparities. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la)
Strengthen their capacity for meaningful self-expression and impactful creative communication through the thoughtful and purposeful use of various artistic mediums to explore and respond to the complexities of inequality. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la)
Gain increased confidence in their ability to contribute to positive change and advocate for social justice within their communities and the wider world using artistic means. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la)
Action
Identify age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote equality and activelyreduce inequalities within their schools, communities, and beyond, considering systemic change and individual agency. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creative skills and artistic voices to advocate for social justice, raise awareness about inequality, and challenge discriminatory practices. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
Reflect thoughtfully on their evolving role as engaged global citizens in creating a more equitable and inclusive world where everyone has the opportunity to thrive, fostering a personal commitment to lifelong advocacy. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

Action Planning
Empowering youth to become active agents of change in reducing inequality within and among countries. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can congtribute to a larger global impact.

Examples:

Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
Practice Inclusive Language: Be mindful of the words you use, ensuring they are respectful and inclusive of all people, regardless of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion, or econimic status.
Challenge Bias: Pay genuine attention to diverse perspectives, especially from those whose experiences differ from your own, to build empathy and understanding.
Advocate for Fairness: If you witness discrimination or unfair treatment, find a safe way to speak up or inform a trusted adult. Your voice can make a difference in promoting equal opportunity.
Suppor Equitable Businesses: When making purchasing decisions, consider supporting businesses that are known for fair labor practices, equal pay, and inclusive hiring, aligning with decent work principles and reducing inequalities of outcome.
Share Resources: If you have access to learning materials, study guides, or opportunities, offer to share them with classmates who might benefit.
Promote Inclusivity in Groups: Ensure that everyone in group projects or activities has a chance to contribute, is heard, and feels valued.
Learn About Diverse Cultures: Seek out opportunities to learn about different cultures, traditions, and backgrounds to foster cross-cultural understanding and reduce prejudice.
Be Mindful of Global Connections: Consider how your choices as a consumer can impact workers and economies in other countries, and support fair trade practices.

Holistic Actions
Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice):
Use visual art, creative writing, dance, drama, or music, to create impactful pieces that illuminate hidden inequalities and promote social justice.
Prepare informative presentations or workshops incorporating creative elements from the lessons to educate others about Global Goal 10 and inspire them to take action.
Join or initiate campaigns advocating for policies that promote equality, eliminate discriminatory laws, or strengthen social protection for vulnerable groups.
Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community):
Research and connect with local organizations that work to reduce inequality in your community, such as food banks, shelters, disability advocacy groups, or organizations supporting migrant families.
Work with school leaders or community groups to identify and address barriers to inclusion (e.g., accessibility issues, discriminatory practices, lack of representation).
Plan and execute events that highlight specific local inequalities.
Patronize local businesses known for fair wages, inclusive hiring, and community engagement, contributing to a more equitable local economy.
Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
Investigate the statistics and impacts of income inequality both within countries and globally.
Explore the reasons for and challenges faced by migrants and refugees globally, including issues like recruitment costs and safe passage.
Investigate how official development assistance and financial flows are directed to developing countries to reduce inequality and the impact of remittance costs.
Learn about the efforts to enhance the representation and voice of developing countries in international and financial institutions.
