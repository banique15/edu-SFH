## Objectives

### Knowledge
- Identify and differentiate between the various interconnected aspects of water and sanitation, including universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water, adequate and equitable sanitation and hygiene, improved water quality, and sustainable water management practices. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Recognize the critical interconnectedness of water and sanitation with fundamental aspects of human health, the delicate balance of ecosystems, and overall global wellbeing and sustainable development. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Understand the complex global challenges related to water and sanitation, including increasing water scarcity, the pervasive impact of pollution, and the stark realities of unequal access affecting vulnerable populations and regions. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

### Discourse
- Analyze and interpret the diverse ways in which various art forms (dance, visual art, creative writing, music, drama) can be powerfully used to express profound emotions, raise critical awareness, and advocate for meaningful change in relation to the urgent global issues of water and sanitation. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)
- Engage thoughtfully and respectfully in meaningful discussions about the multifaceted challenges of water and sanitation, exploring diverse perspectives, lived experiences, and potential solutions from individuals, communities, and global organizations. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Express their own nuanced thoughts, feelings, and developing ideas about the significance of water and sanitation through purposeful and impactful creative expression and innovative artistic processes across a range of mediums. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr5.1.la)

### Attitudes
- Develop a deepened sense of empathy and compassion for the daily struggles faced by individuals and communities lacking access to clean water and adequate sanitation, fostering a more humane and understanding perspective on these fundamental human rights. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Cultivate a strong sense of personal and collective responsibility for the urgent need for water conservation and the adoption of sustainable water management practices to protect this precious resource for current and future generations. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
- Foster a sense of global citizenship and a personal desire to contribute actively to creating a more equitable and sustainable world where clean water and adequate sanitation are available and accessible to all. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)

### Capacity
- Enhance their creativity, collaboration, and articulate communication skills through engaging in in-depth artistic explorations and the development of impactful group projects that address water and sanitation themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
- Develop advanced critical thinking skills to thoroughly analyze the root causes of water and sanitation challenges, evaluate the effectiveness and ethical implications of potential solutions, and understand the complexities of global water resource management. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Gain a deeper understanding of the unique and powerful role of the arts in promoting critical awareness, fostering empathy, challenging dominant narratives, and inspiring meaningful action on complex environmental and social issues like water and sanitation. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)

### Action
- Identify and critically evaluate a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to conserve water, reduce pollution, and promote sanitation in their daily lives, schools, communities, and beyond. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Explore and develop meaningful ways to contribute to the achievement of Global Goal 6 at local, national, and global levels through advocacy, awareness campaigns, and direct engagement in relevant initiatives. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
- Use artistic expression across various mediums as a powerful tool for advocacy, raising public awareness about the urgent need for clean water and sanitation for all, and inspiring positive change in attitudes and behaviors. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la)
- Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a more sustainable and equitable world with clean water and sanitation for all, fostering a sense of sustained engagement and responsible global citizenship. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

## Action Planning

Empowering youth to become active agents of change in the pursuit of ensuring availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can contribute to a larger global impact.

### Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
- Conserve Water at Home: Be mindful of water usage by taking shorter showers, turning off the tap while brushing teeth, and fixing leaky faucets.
- Reduce Plastic Waste: Opt for reusable water bottles and avoid single-use plastic items to minimize pollution that impacts water quality.
- Learn and Share Water Conservation Techniques: Research different methods for conserving water and share these tips with family and friends to spread awareness and encourage wider adoption.
- Support Good Hygiene Practices: Practice and promote handwashing with soap and water to contribute to better sanitation and hygiene.
- Dispose of Waste Properly: Always dispose of trash and hazardous materials responsibly to prevent them from contaminating water sources.
- Be Mindful of Chemical Use: Reduce the use of harsh chemicals in cleaning and gardening that can end up in wastewater and harm water quality.
- Report Water Pollution: If you see signs of water pollution (e.g., unusual colors, strong odors in local water bodies), report it to relevant authorities or environmental groups.
- Choose Sustainable Products: Be aware of the “water footprint” of products you buy and support companies that prioritize sustainable water practices in their production.
- Engage in Water-Related Discussions: Talk with friends and family about the importance of clean water and sanitation, sharing facts and personal reflections from this curriculum.
- Appreciate Water as a Resource: Take a moment to appreciate the availability of clean water in your daily life, fostering a sense of gratitude and responsibility for this precious resource.
- Support Local Clean-up Efforts: Participate in  local river, lake, or beach clean-ups to directly improve water quality in your community.
- Understand Your Local Water Source: Learn where your drinking water comes from and how it is treated, fostering a deeper connection to your local water infrastructure.

### Holistic Actions

#### Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice)
- Use visual art, dance, drama, creative writing, or music to create impactful pieces that raise awareness about Global Goal 6.
- Prepare informative presentations or workshops incorporating the creative elements from the lesson to educate other students about Global Goal 6 and inspire them to take action.
- Write persuasive letters to local, state, or national representatives, advocating for policies that support clean water and sanitation initiatives.
- Research and support organizations working to provide clean water and sanitation to communities in need through fundraising events, awareness campaigns, or direct donations.

#### Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community)
- Lead or participate in school-wide or community-wide campaigns to promote water conservation, encouraging practices like responsible irrigation and leak detection.
- Join or organize community clean-up events for local riviers, lakes, streams, or public spaces to prevent pollution from reaching water sources.
- Invite local water utility representatives or environmental experts to speak about local water challenges, management practices, and career opportunities in the water sector.
- Design and implement small-scale sustainable water projects at school or in the community, such as a rainwater harvesting system for a garden or a demonstration of greywater recycling.
- Explore ways to support local administrative units in establishing and operating policies and procedures for community participatgion in water and sanitation management.

#### Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
- Deepen understanding of the complexities of water scarcity, pollution, and unequal access in different parts of the world through independent research and discussions.
- Learn about international cooperation and capacity-building support initiatives for developing countries in water and sanitation, such as water harvesting, desalination, and wastewater treatment technologies.
- Stay informed about current events, breakthroughs, and ongoing challenges related to global water and sanitation through reputable news sources and reports from organizations like the UN and World Health Organizations (WHO).
- Explore opportunities to connect with students from other countries (e.g., through virtual exchange programs) to learn about their unique water and sanitation challenges and solutions.
- Reflect on how Global Goal 6 is interconnected with other Global Goals for Sustainable Development and the broader implications for global wellbeing.
