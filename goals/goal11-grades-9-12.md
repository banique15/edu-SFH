GRADES 9-12

Objectives
In this curriculum, students will:

Knowledge
Identify and articulate the diverse challenges and opportunities related to creating inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable cities and communities, as outlined in UN Global Goal 11. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Understand the specific targets and indicators within Global Goal 11, including those related to affordable housing, sustainable transportation, inclusive urbanization, cultural and natural heritage protection, disaster resilience, urban environmental impact, green and public spaces, urban-rural linkages, and sustainble building. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la)
Recognize the interconnectedness of these targets and how they collectively contribute to the overall goal of comprehensive sustainable urban development and improved human wellbeing. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
Understand the history and cultural context of various art forms (music, visual art, poerty, dance, theater) and analyze how these artistic expressions have been utilized to voice social issues and promote change in urban environments. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
Discourse
Analyze and interpret how various art forms (music, visual art, poetry, dance/movement, theater) can be powerfully used to express emotions, raise critical awareness, and advocate for meaningful change related to urban sustainability and its challenges. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)
Engage thoughtfully and respectfully in critical discussions about the complexities of urban challenges and the potential solutions related to achieving the targets of Global Goal 11, considering diverse perspectives and lived experiences. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
Express their own nuanced and developing thoughts, feelings, and innovative ideas about sustainable cities and communities through purposeful and impactful creative expression in music, art, poetry, dance, and theater. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
Learn how to effectively tell and share their own stories, and actively listen to and interpret the stories of others, in order to gain a deeper and more empathetic understanding of the multifaceted issues surrounding sustainable cities and human settlements. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la)
Attitudes
Develop a deepened sense of empathy and compassion for individuals and communities facing diverse urban challenges, such as inadequate housing, lack of access to transportation, lack of safe public spaces, and environmental degradation in cities. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
Cultivate a strong sense of personal and collective responsibility for their role in creating and contributing to more inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable cities and communities for all inhabitants. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
Foster a robust sense of global citizenship and a personal desire to actively contribute to achieving the targets of Global Goal 11, recognizing the universal need for livable and equitable urban environments. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
Challenge preconceived notions and stereotypes about urban life, and proactively explore the complexities, diversity, and dynamic nature of city living. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la)
Capacity
Enhance their creativity, collaboration, and articulate communication skills through engaging in rigorous group projects in music composition, visual art creation, poetry writing, choreography, and dramatic performance. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
Develop advanced critical thinking skills to thoroughly analyze the root causes of urban challenges (e.g., rapid urbanization, infrastructure deficits, social exclusion) and to critically evaluate the effectiveness and ethical implications of potential solutions. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
Gain a deeper understanding of the unique and powerful role of the arts in promoting social change, fostering community engagement, challenging societal norms, and inspiring meaningful action towards sustainable urban development. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
Develop the capacity to strategically use artistic expression as versatile a tool for advocacy, community awareness, and public dialogue on issues related to sustainable cities and human settlements. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la)
Action
Identify and implement a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to contribute to achieving the targets of Global Goal 11 in their local communities and broader urban environments. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creative skills and artistic voices to raise awareness about urban sustainability issues, advocate for positive change, and inspire others to engage in local and global efforts. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a more inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable cities and communities for all, fostering a sense of sustained engagement and responsible global citizenship. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
Create an action plan to help their local community and the world become more sustainable, and to effectively raise awareness about the issues surrounding sustainable cities through artistic interventions. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)

Action Planning
Empowering youth to become active agents of change in creating inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable cities and human settlements. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can congtribute to a larger global impact.

Examples:

Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
Support Local Businesses and Services: Prioritize supporting businesses and services within your community that contribute to its vibrancy and sustainability. This helps strengthen the local economy and reduces the need for long-distance travel and resource consumption.
Opt for Sustainable Transportation: Choose to walk, bike, or use public transport whenever possible. This reduces traffic congestion, lowers carbon emissions, and promotes healthier lifestyles, contributing to cleaner air and more efficient cities.
Practice Responsible Waste Management: Actively participate in recycling gand composting programs available in your area. Reduce your consumption of single-use plastics and other disposable items to minimize landfill waste.
Conserve Resources at Home: Be mindful of your energy and water consumption. Turn off lights when leaving a room, unplug electronics when not in use, and fix leaky faucets to save resources.
Explore and Appreciate Green Spaces: Spend time in local parks, community gardens, and public spaces. This helps foster an appreciation for these vital areas and encourages their maintenance and expansion.
Learn About Local History and Culture: Visit local museums, historical sites, and cultural centers. Understanding your community’s heritage helps foster a sense of belonging and encourages preservation efforts.
Engage in Community Discussions: Pay attention to local news and discussions about urban planning, development, and community issues. Stay informed about how decisions are made that affect your neighborhood.
Be a Considerate Neighbor: Contribute to a safe and inclusive community by being respectful of others, participating in neighborhood watch programs in available, and fostering a sense of mutual support.
Share Your Ideas for a Better City: Don’t hesitate to share your thoughts and innovative ideas for improving your city or community with friends, family, teachers, or local officials.
Holistic Actions
Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice):
Utilize visual art, creative writing, dance, drama, and/or music to produce impactful pieces that raise awareness about specific issues related to Global Goal 11, such as the need for affordable housing, sustainable transportation, or accessible green spaces.
Develop and deliver presentations or workshops, incorporating the creative elements from the lessons, to inform others about the challenges and opportunities related to sustainable cities and communities.
Research local urban planning initiatives, public transport projects, or green space development plans. Write letters, emails, or participate in public meetings to advocate for policies that align with the goals of Global Goal 11.
Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community):
Look for opportunities to volunteer with local organizations that focus on urban improvement, such as community clean-ups, park revitalization efforts, or initiatives supporting vulnerable populations.
Attend local city council meetings, zoning board hearings, or community planning workshops. Your presence and input can help shape decisions that promote inclusive and sustainable urbanization.
Join or start a community garden, participate in tree-planting events, or advocate for more green infrastructure in your neighborhood.
Participate in surveys, attend public forums, or join local groups that advocate for improved public transportation routes, accessibility, and affordability.
Volunteer with or support local museums, historical societies, or cultural preservation groups to help safeguard your community’s unique heritage.
Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
Investigate how different cities around the world are addressing challenges like rapid urbanization, climate change impacts, or informal settlements. Learn from their successes and failures.
Research countries or cities that are recognized leaders in sustainable urban development, focusing on innovative solutions for housing, transportation, and environmental management.
Follow news and updates from the United Nations and other international organizations on progress towards Global Goal 11 and related sustainable development goals.
Investigate how communities globally are developing and implementing disaster risk reduction strategies and adapting to climate change, particularly in vulnerable areas.
