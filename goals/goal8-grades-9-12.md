## Objectives

### Knowledge
- Develop a comprehensive understanding of the concept of “decent work” as defined by the ILO and its crucial importance for individual dignity, community wellbeing, and sustainable economic development. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Gain a critical awareness of the complex global challenges and diverse opportunities related to employment, fundamental labor rights, the pursuit of sustainable economic development, and the principles of social justice in the world of work. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Explore the intricate interconnectedness of sustained and inclusive economic growth, social progress that benefits all, and environmental sustainability in shaping a just and equitable future of work. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
- Learn about the United Nations Global Goal 8 and its specific role in promoting decent work for all, fostering sustainable economic growth, and addressing issues like youth employment, forced labor, and fair wages on a global scale. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la)
- Develop foundational knowledge of various creative disciplines, including drama, visual art, creative writing, dance, and music, and their potential as powerful tools for exploring and communicating complex socio-economic issues. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la)

### Discourse
- Analyze and interpret diverse forms of creative expression (drama, visual art, poetry, music, dance) to identify and critically examine the underlying themes, powerful messages, and varied perspectives related to decent work, economic growth, and the experiences of workers. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)
- Engage thoughtfully in respectful and nuanced discussions about complex social and economic issues and economic issues surrounding the world of work, demonstrating active listening skills and a genuine respect for diverse viewpoints and lived experiences. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Articulate their own informed ideas, insightful perspectives, and critical reflections on the challenges and opportunities within the realm of decent work and economic growth through both clear verbal and impactful nonverbal communication. (NCCAS: Pr5.1.la)
- Collaborate effectively and creatively with peers to generate, develop, and share meaningful artistic projects that explore and respond to the multifaceted themes of Global Goal 8. (NCCAS:Cr3 .1.la, Pr6.1.la)

### Attitudes
- Cultivate a deep sense of empathy and compassion for individuals and diverse communities facing significant challenges related to unemployment, precarious work, poverty, and economic inequality across the globe. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Challenge their own and societal stereotypes, biases, and preconceived notions related to different types of work, the diverse experience of workers, and the complex realities of varying economic circumstances. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Develop a strong sense of personal and collective responsibility for actively promoting fair labor practices, making ethical consumption choices, and advocating for sustainable economic development that benefits all members of society and respects the planet. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)
- Foster a sense of global citizenship and a personal commitment to taking informed and meaningful action to contribute to a more just and equitable world where everyone has access to decent work and opportunities for sustained and inclusive economic growth.

### Capacity
- Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, problem-solving, and articulate communication skills through active and engaged participation in a variety of creative activities and collaborative projects centered around the themes of Global Goal 8. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
- Develop their analytical abilities to interpret information and diverse perspectives from various sources, including dramatic scenes, visual artwork, dance, musical compositions, creative writing, and real-world examples related to the world of work and economic systems. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la)
- Strengthen their capacity for meaningful self-expression and impactful creative communication through the thoughtful and purposeful use of various artistic mediums to explore and respond to the complexities of decent work and economic growth. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la)
- Gain increased confidence in utilizing the arts as powerful tools for effective communication, persuasive advocacy, and inspiring positive action on critical global issues. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la)

### Action
- Identify and critically evaluate a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote decent work and economic growth within their schools, communities, and beyond. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their creative skills and artistic voices to advocate for social and economic justice in the world of work. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
- Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role in creating a more equitable and sustainable world where everyone has access to decent work and opportunities for sustained and inclusive economic growth. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

## Action Planning

Empowering youth to become active agents of change in the pursuit of promoting sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can contribute to a larger global impact.

### Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
- Practice Responsible Consumption: Think about where your clothes, electronics, and food come from. Support brands that have ethical labor practices and sustainable production methods.
- Value All Work: Recognize and appreciate the dignity in all types of work, from service industries to skilled trades, and challenge biases against certain professions.
- Develop Your Skills: Actively engage in learning new skills (both academic and vocational) that can contribute to a productive workforce in the future.
- Be a Responsible Digital Citizen: Understand how your online activities can contribute to or detract from a positive digital economy, and protect yourself from exploitative practices.
- Support Local Businesses: Choose to buy from local, small businesses to support entrepreneurship and job creation in your community.
- Minimize Waste: Reduce, reuse, and recycle to improve resource efficiency in consumption and production.
- Champion Inclusivity: Be welcoming and inclusive of all people in group settings, extracurricular activities, and future workplaces, recognizing diverse talents and abilities.
- Educate Yourself on Labor Rights: Learn about basic labor rights, such as fair wages, safe working conditions, and the right to organize, to better understand what constitutes “decent work.”
- Speak Up Against Injustice: If you witness unfair treatment or exploitation in any context, find a safe way to speak up or inform a trusted adult.
- Share Knowledge on Fair Practices: If you learn about ethical companies or fair labor practices, share that information with friends and family to raise awareness.

### Holistic Actions

#### Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice)
- Use visual art, creative writing, dance, drama, or music to create impactful pieces that raise awareness about Global Goal 8.
- Prepare informative presentations or workshops incorporating the creative elements from the lessons to educate others about Global Goal 8 and inspire them to take action.
- Research and promote ethical brands that adhere to fair labor practices and avoid child labor.

#### Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community)
- Actively seek out and patronize local micro, small, and medium sized enterprises (MSMEs) in your community.
- Offer your time to local organizations that support workforce development, youth employment programs, or provide financial literacy training.
- Support or organize fair trade events in your school or community to promote equitable trade practices for producers in developing countries.
- Investigate employment opportunities and labor conditions in your local area, focusing on inclusivity for youth, persons with disabilities, and ensuring equal pay.

#### Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
- Research the origins of common products to understand the global supply chains and identify potential issues like child labor or unfair wages.
- Investigate global and national strategies aimed at reducing youth unemployment, including initiatives by the International Labour Organization (ILO).
- Explore the importance of access to banking and financial services for all in promoting economic empowerment, especially in developing countries.
- Identify and engage with international organizations working on labor rights, anti-slavery, or sustainable economic development.
