GRADES 9-12

Objectives
In this curriculum, students will:

Knowledge
Identify and understand the core concepts of Global Goal 16: Promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable, and inclusive institutions at all levels. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Recognize the multifaceted nature of peace, justice, and strong institutions, including the reduction of violence, eradication of exploitation, promotion of the rule of law, reduction of corruption, and enhancement of inclusive decision-making. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Understand the diverse impacts of instability, lack of justice, and weak institutions on individuals, communities, and global development. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Explore how different art forms (music, visual art, creative writing, dance, and drama) can effectively communicate complex information about peace, justice, and governance, express related emotions, and inspire action. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Gain knowledge of specific real-world examples and historical contexts related to struggles for peace, justice, and strong institutions. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la) 
Discourse
Analyze and interpret various artistic works (songs, poems, visual art, dance performances, dramatic interpretations) that explore themes of peace, justice, conflict, corruption, human rights, and governance. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la) 
Engage thoughtfully and respectfully in informed discussions about the scientific, social, ethical, and artistic dimensions of peace, justice, and governance, considering diverse perspectives and viewpoints on both challenges and solutions. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Express their own nuanced thoughts, complex feelings, and innovative ideas about peace, justice, and effective institutions through purposeful and impactful creative artistic expression, utilizing a variety of media and techniques. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr5.1.la) 
Learn how specific art forms can serve as powerful platforms for public discourse, allowing for the articulation of urgent messages and the fostering of empathy around issues of peace and justice. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 
Attitudes
Develop a heightened sense of urgency and personal responsibility regarding the imperative need for peace, justice, and strong institutions at individual, community, national, and global levels. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 
Cultivate deep empathy and compassion for individuals and communities disproportionately affected by violence, injustice, and weak governance. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Challenge apathy, denial, and misinformation related to social and political injustices, promoting critical thinking and evidence-based understanding. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Foster a resilient sense of hope and empowerment, inspiring students to believe in their individual and collective ability to make a significant positive difference in building more peaceful, just, and inclusive societies. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 
Capacity
Enhance their creativity, collaboration, and articulate communication skills through active participation in collaborative artistic projects and performances focused on themes of peace, justice, and governance. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la) 
Develop their ability to strategically use skills artistic techniques and various media to effectively communicate complex social and political information, evoke stong emotional responses, and convey urgent messages. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la) 
Strengthen their analytical skills to discern the underlying messages and calls to action within artistic expressions related to peace, justice, and governance. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la) 
Gain a deeper understanding of the unique and powerful role of the arts in raising critical awareness, fostering dialogue, inspiring collective action, and promoting social change for a more just and peaceful world. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 
Action
Identify and critically evaluate a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote peace, justice, and strong institutions within their schools, communities, and beyond. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la) 
Explore and develop meaningful ways to engage in advocacy and activism to support policies and initiatives that promote peace, justice and good governance, leveraging their artistic skills. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la) 
Use their chosen art form as a powerful tool for raising public awareness, influencing public opinion, and mobilizing others to take concrete action to combat violence, injustice, and corruption. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la) 
Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role as active and engaged global citizens in addressing social and political injustices and contributing to creating a more peaceful, just, and inclusive future for all. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 

Action Planning
Empowering youth to become active agents of change in promoting peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, providing access to justice for all, and building effective, accountable, and inclusive institutions at all levels. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can congtribute to a larger global impact.

Examples:

Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
Practice Empathy and Respect: Actively listen to others, seek to understand different perspectives, and treat everyone with kindness and respect, even when you disagree. This fosters an inclusive environment.
Resolve Conflicts Peacefully: Learn and practice conflict resolution skills in your daily interactions, seeking constructive ways to address disagreements without resorting to violence or aggression.
Speak Up Against Injustice: When you witness bullying, discrimination, or unfair treatment, speak up or seek help from a trusted adult. Silence can perpetuate injustice.
Be a Reponsible Digital Citizen: Use online platofrms respectfully, avoid spreading misinformation, and report cyberbullying or hate speech. Promote positive and inclusive online spaces.
Educate Yourself on Rights: Learn about your own rights and the rights of others, including fundamental freedoms and access to information.
Support Local Laws and Rules: Understand and abide by the laws and rules of your school and community. This contributes to the overall rule of law and order.
Participate in Democratic Processes: If and when old enough, vote in elections. If not, learn about local government, attend school council meetings, or participate in student government.
Be Accountable: Take responsibility for your actions and learn from your mistakes. Encourage accountability in others.
Promote Inclusivity in Groupus: Actively invite and include everfyone in social activities, group projects, and discussions, ensuring all voices are heard.
Question Information: Develop critical thinking skills to evaluate information, especially from social media, to identify misinformation or propaganda that can fuel conflict.
Holistic Actions
Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice):
Utilize visual art, creative writing, dance, drama, and/or music to produce impactful pieces that raise awareness about violence, injustice, lack of access to justice, or the importance of strong institutions.
Prepare and deliver informative presentations or workshops incorporating the creative elements from the lessons, to educate your peers and community about Global Goal 16 and inspire action for peace and justice.
Use storytelling, poetry, or dramatic performance to share personal connections to issues of peace, justice, or human rights, fostering empathy and understanding among your audience.
Research issues of corruption or lack of transparency in your local community or broader society. Use your voice to advocate for greater accountability in public institutions.
Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community):
Connect with local organizations working on violence prevention, conflict resolution, or peacebuilding programs in your community.
Look for opportunities to volunteer with legal aid services, human rights organizations, or groups supporting victims of violence or exploitation.
Attend town hall meetings, community forums, or school board meetings to understand local decision-making processes and advocate for inclusive and representative governance.
Work with school administration and student councils to implement policies that promote non-discrimination and ensure all students feel safe and included.
Research how your local government operates, who your representatives are, and how you can engage with them to advocate for justice and accountability.
Volunteer with or support organizations that aim to end abuse, exploitation, and trafficking of children.
Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
Research ongoing conflicts around the world and the efforts of international organizations and NGOs in peacebuilding and conflict resolution.
Explore the role of international courts and legal frameworks in ensuring justice for all and promoting the rule of law at a global level.
Research the participation of developing countries in international organizations and how their voices are heard in global decision-making.
Learn about global efforts to reduce illicit financial and arms flows and combat organized crime.
Study the work of human rights advocates and journalists around the world and the importance of protecting fundamental freedoms.
Learn about the importance of birth registration and legal identity for all, especially in developing countries.
Investigate international initiatives and policies aimed at substantially reducing corruption and bribery in all their forms.
