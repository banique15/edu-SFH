## Objectives

### Knowledge
- Understand the key targets and indicators of the United Nations' Global Goal 4: Quality Education, recognizing its multifaceted nature encompassing access, equity, quality, and lifelong learnings. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Recognize the diverse and interconnected barriers to achieving quality education worldwide, including poverty, discrimination (based on gender, disability, ethnicity, etc.), conflict, lack of resources, and inadequate infrastructure. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Identify and analyze potential solutions and actions at individual, community, national, and global levels to address these challenges and promote educational equity and inclusive learning opportunities for all. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

### Discourse
- Analyze and interpret the powerful messages and diverse themes related to education, its importance, its challenges, and its transformative potential as expressed in various art forms. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la)
- Engage thoughtfully and respectfully in critical discussions about the crucial role of education in individual empowerment, social progress, and sustainable development, exploring different perspectives and lived experiences. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Express their own informed perspectives, creative ideas, and insightful critiques about the state of education and their visions for its future through purposeful and impactful creative expression. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr51.la)

### Attitudes
- Develop a deepened sense of empathy and understanding towards individuals and communities facing significant educational challenges and barriers to accessing quality learning opportunities. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Challenge their own and societal stereotypes, biases, and assumptions related to education, access to learning, and the potential of all learners, fostering a more inclusive and equitable worldview. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la)
- Cultivate a strong sense of personal empowerment and agency, recognizing their own capacity to contribute to positive change in the field of education, both within their immediate communities and on a larger scale. (NCCAS: Cn11.1la)

### Capacity
- Enhance their critical thinking and analytical skills through analysis, thoughtful brainstorming, and the creation of creative pieces. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la)
- Develop their creativity, collaboration, and effective performance skills through engaging in group arts activities and presentations. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la)
- Gain a deeper understanding of the power of the arts for raising awareness, inspiring critical dialogue, and advocating for social justice and educational equity. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la)

### Action
- Identify and critically evaluate a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote quality education, inclusivity, and access to learning opportunities within their local communities and beyond. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la)
- Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their voices, creativity, and artistic skills to advocate for educational equity, challenge systemic barriers, and raise awareness about the importance of Global Goal 4. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la)
- Reflect thoughtfully on their role as engaged global citizens in supporting the achievement of Global Goal 4, fostering a personal commitment to lifelong learning and promoting educational opportunities for all. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

## Action Planning

Empowering youth to become active agents of change in the pursuit of inclusive and equitable quality education for all. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can contribute to a larger global impact.

### Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
- Embrace Curiosity: Ask questions, explore topics that interest you beyond the classroom, and never stop learning. This embodies the “lifelong learning” aspect of Global Goal 4.
- Value All Forms of Learning: Recognize that learning happens in many ways, through school, hobbies, conversations, and experiences. Appreciate diverse ways of acquiring knowledge and skills.
- Seek Understanding: Focus on truly understanding concepts rather than just memorizing. This contributes to “relevant and effective learning outcomes.”
- Help a Classmate: If you understand a concept well, offer to explain it to a classmate who might be struggling. This promotes inclusive learning within your immediate environment.
- Read Widely: Explore different genres and topics through reading. This improves literacy and expands your understanding of the world.
- Listen Actively to Others: Pay attention to different perspectives in class discussions and value the contributions of everyone, regardless of their background or perceived abilities.
- Challenge Your Own Biases: Be aware of your own assumptions and stereotypes about who can learn waht, and actively work to overcome them.
- Speak Up Against Exclusion: If you witness someone being excluded or treated unfairly in an educational setting, find a safe way to speak up on inform a trusted adult.
- Value Diverse Skills and Talents: Recognize that intelligence and capability come in many forms, not just academic achievements. Appreciate the unique talents of your peers.
- Be Inclusive in Group Work: Make sure everyone in your group has a chance to contribute their ideas and participate fully.
- Take Care of School Resources: Treat books, equipment, and facilities with respect so they can benefit all students.
- Conserve Resources: Be mindful of paper usage, electricity, and water in the school environment. Sustainable practices support better learning environments for everyone.
- Share Learning Materials: If you have resources like extra notes or study guides, offer to share them with classmates who might need them.
- Advocate for a Better Learning Environment: If you notice something that could make yoru school more inclusive, safe, or effective for learning, voice your concerns respectfully through appropriate channels.
- Stay Informed: Read news and articles about global issues, including educagtional challenges in different parts of the world.
- Share Interesting Fact: If you learn something interesting about education globally, share it with your friends or family to raise awareness.
- Support Ethical Consumption: Be mindful of the products you buy and support companies that prioritize ethical practices, which can indirectly contribute to fair labor and educational opportunities.
- Talk About the Importance of Education: Have conversations with your friends and family about why education is important for individuals and society as a whole.

### Holistic Actions

#### Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice)
- Organize short presentations or discussions during lunch breaks, club meetings, or even within classes to raise awareness about Global Goal 4 and specific educational issues.
  - **Concrete Action:** Form a student club/group dedicated to organizing these talks at least once per semester.
- Participate in letter-writing campaigns or petition drives advocating for policies that support quality and equitable eduction, both locally and globally. Research organizations working on education rights and join their initiatives.
  - **Concrete Action:** Dedicate one hour per month to participating in such campaigns.
- Use creative skills to create pieces that highlight the importance of education or depict the challenges faced by those without access. These can be displayed in school, shared online, or even used to support fundraising efforts.
  - **Concrete Action:** Organize a school-wide “Art for Education” showcase once a year.

#### Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community)
- Volunteer to tutor younger students in their school or community who may be struggling academically. This directly contributes to Target 4.1 by supporting learning outcomes.
  - **Concrete Action:** Dedicate 1-2 hours per week to tutoring.
- Organize and lead workshops for peers or younger students to share valuable skills (e.g., coding, digital literacy, art, music, study skills). This connects to Target 4.4 by promoting relevant skills development.
  - **Concrete Action:** Each student with a specific skill offers to lead at least one workshop per semester.
- Research and connect with local organizations that work to improve education access or quality in their community. Offer volunteer time or organize small fundraising events to support their work.
  - **Concrete Action:** The class collectively identifies one local organiztion to support each year.
- Organize drives within the school or community to collect essential school supplies for students in need, both locally and/or globally through partnerships with international organizations.
  - **Concrete Action:** Conduct a school supply drive at the beginning of each academic year.

#### Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
- Explore opportunities to connect with students in other countries through online platforms or pen pal programs to learn about their educational experiences and challenges. This fosters understanding of global disparities related to Target 4.5.
  - **Concrete Action:** The class explores and participates in at least one virtual exchange project per year.
- Organize screenings of documentaries or films that highlight global education issues followed by thoughtful discussions. This can deepen understanding of of the complexities surrounding Global Goal 4.
  - **Concrete Action:** Host at least one screening and discussion per semester.
- Follow and engage with individuals and organizations working on global education on social media to stay informed and inspired.
  - **Concrete Action:** Each student identifies and follows at least two relevant accounts.
