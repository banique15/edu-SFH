GRADES 9-12

Objectives
In this curriculum, students will:
Knowledge
Understand the critical role of strong global partnerships in achieving the Global Goals for Sustainable Development, recognizing their multifaceted nature across finance, technology, capacity-building, trade, data, and multi-stakeholder collaboration. (NCCAS: Cr1.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Recognize the diverse and interconnected challenges to effective global partnerships, including funding gaps, unequal access to science, technology and innovation, capacity limitations in developing countries, complex trade barriers, and issues of policy coherence. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Identify and analyze a range of potential solutions and actions at individual, community, national, and global levels to strengthen partnerships and accelerate progress towards the Global Goals, fostering a more equitable and effective system of global cooperation. (NCCAS: Cn10.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Discourse
Analyze and interpret how various art forms (visual art, creative writing, dance, drama, and music) can powerfully express ideas, raise awareness, and advocate for enhanced global cooperation and equitable partnerships. (NCCAS: Re7.1.la, Re8.1.la) 
Engage thoughtfully and respectfully in critical discussions about the crucial role of partnerships in global development, exploring different perspectives on fairness, effectiveness, and shared responsibility among nations and stakeholders. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Express their own informed perspectives, creative ideas, and insightful critiques about the state of global partnerships and their visions for more effective and inclusive collaboration through purposeful and impactful creative expression across various artistic mediums. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr5.1.la) 
Attitudes
Develop a deepened sense of empathy and understanding toward countries and communities facing significant resource and capacity challenges in achieving the Global Goals for Sustainable Development, fostering a more compassionate global perspective. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Challenge their own and societal assumptions and biases related to international aid, trade imbalances, technology transfer, and the roles of different nations in global partnerships, fostering a more equitable and collaborative worldview. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn10.1.la) 
Cultivate a strong sense of personal empowerment and agency, recognizing their own capacity to contribute to positive change in fostering stronger, more equitable, and more effective global partnerships for sustainable development. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 
Foster a robust sense of global citizenship and a sustained commitment to taking informed and creative action to support the achievement of Global Goal 17, promoting active collaboration and mutual support for a shared future. (NCCAS: Cn11.1.la) 
Capacity
Enhance their creativity, critical thinking, problem solving, and articulate communication skills through active and engaged participation in a variety of creative activities and collaborative projects focused on global partnership themes. (NCCAS: Cr2.1.la, Pr5.1.la) 
Develop their analytical abilities to interpret information and diverse perspectives from various artistic sources, written texts, dance performances, dramatic interpretations, and musical compositions related to international cooperation and development. (NCCAS: Re8.1.la) 
Strengthen their capacity for meaningful self-expression and impactful creative communication through the thoughtful and purposeful use of various artistic mediums to explore and respond to the complexities of global partnerships (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la) 
Gain increased confidence in using the arts as powerful tools for effective communication, persuasive advocacy, and inspiring positive action on critical global issues related to international cooperation and sustainable development. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la) 
Action
Identify and critically evaluate a range of age-appropriate and impactful actions they can take to promote stronger and more equitable global partnerships within their local communities, schools, and beyond. (NCCAS: Pr6.1.la, Cn11.1.la) 
Explore and develop meaningful ways to use their voices, creativity, and artistic skills to advocate for fair trade, responsible technology transfer, increased development assistance, enhanced statistical capacity, and other aspects of Global Goal 17. (NCCAS: Cr3.1.la, Pr6.1.la) 
Reflect thoughtfully on their individual and collective role as engaged global citizens in supporting the achievement of Global Goal 17, fostering a personal commitment to active collaboration and promoting partnerships for sustainable development globally. (NCCAS: Re9.1.la, Cn11.1.la)

Action Planning
Empowering youth to become active agents of change in strengthening the means of implementation and revitalizing the Global Partnership for Sustainable Development. Recognizing that even small, consistent actions can congtribute to a larger global impact.

Examples:

Small, Everyday Actions to Inspire Change
Stay Informed: Keep yourself updated on global issues, international agreements, and the work of organizations like the UN. Understanding the context is crucial for effective action.
Support Fair Trade: Choose products that are ethically sourced and traded, ensuring fair wages and working conditions for producers in developing countries.
Be a Responsible Consumer: Reduce your consumption of goods and services, and choose sustainable options whenever possible. This lessens the strain on global resources and promotes fair distribution.
Use Technology Responsibly: Utilize technology to connect with others, share information, and learn about global issues, but be mindful of digital equity and access.
Advocate for Ethical Practices: Support businesses and organizations that prioritize ethical labor practices, environmental sustainability, and transparency.
Promote Financial Literacy: Learn about personal finance and responsible investing, and consider supporting financial institutions that prioritize ethical and sustainable practices.
Value Diverse Perspectives: Actively seek out and listen to voices from different cultures and backgrounds. Understanding diverse perspectives is crucial for effective collaboration.
Be a Global Citizen Online: Engage respectfully in online discussions about global issues, challenge misinformation, and promote cross-cultural understanding.
Holistic Actions
Awareness and Advocacy (Using Your Voice):
Utilize visual art, creative writing, dance, drama, and/or music to produce impactful pieces that raise awareness about the importance of international cooperation, fair trade, technology transfer, and other aspects of Global Goal 17.
Develop and deliver presentations, workshops, or public awareness campaigns, incorporating the creative elements from the lessons, to inform others about the challenges and opportunities related to global partnerships.
Research international agreements, trade policies, or development assistance programs. Write letters, emails, or participate in public forums to advocate for policies that align with the goals of Global Goal 17.
Share stories and information about successful partnerships, challenges faced by developing countries, and the need for global cooperation.
Local Engagement and Support (Acting in Your Community):
Research and support local organizations or initiatives that are connected to international development or global partnerships.
Advocate for fair trade practices in your community. Support businesses that sell fair trade products and educate others about their importance.
Connect with people from different cultural backgrounds in your community to foster understanding and build bridges.
Participate in or support exchange programs that connect students and educators from different geographic areas, demographics, and countries.
Encourage your school to incorporate global citizenship education into the curriculum, emphasizing the importance of international cooperation.
Global Citizenship and Learning (Expanding Your Understanding)
Learn about the work of the United Nations, the World Trade Organization, the World Bank, and other international organizations that play a role in global partnerships.
Investigate international trade agreements and their impact on developing countries and sustainable development.
Research how developed countries provide official development assistance (ODA) and the effectiveness of different aid programs.
Explore how technology is shared between developed and developing countries and the challenges in ensuring equitable access.
Research the movement of financial resources between countries, including remittances and foreign investment.
Follow news and updates from reputable sources about international cooperation, trade, development, and global challenges.
